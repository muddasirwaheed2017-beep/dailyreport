IEC + IES standards for Pakistani LED installations

  • IEC 62560 — safety of LED lamps with self-ballasted circuits (the bulb's built-in driver)
  • IEC 62384 — performance requirements for LED modules
  • IEC 61347-2-13 — LED driver standalone safety
  • IEC 62717 — LED module performance (lumens, CCT, CRI)
  • IES LM-79 — lumens measurement methodology
  • IES LM-80 — lumen depreciation over 6,000+ hours (L70 / L80 / L90 rating)

Understanding LED lifespan ratings (L70 / L80 / L90)

LED lights don't fail abruptly — they slowly dim over thousands of hours. The lifespan rating tells you how many hours the LED can run before its output falls below a threshold:

  • L70: hours to 70% of original lumens (most common Pakistani retail spec)
  • L80: hours to 80% — tighter quality standard
  • L90: hours to 90% — premium commercial standard

Tier 3 Pakistani retail typically rates L70 = 25,000-50,000 hours. Tier 1 European / Japanese: L80 = 50,000+ hours. Tier 5 unbranded: often no rating or unverifiable.

Lumens vs Watts — the 2026 buying rule

Pakistani buyers still compare LED prices by wattage (PKR per W), but the right metric is lumens delivered (PKR per 1000 lumens). Reason: LED efficiency varies 80-180 lm/W — you can pay more for an 8W LED that delivers 1,200 lumens than for a 12W LED that delivers 900 lumens.

LED driver protection for commercial / industrial

Pakistani electrical inspection requires LED installations above 50W (commercial) or any LED in a critical safety circuit (hospital, school, mall) to have:

  • Type 2 SPD upstream per IEC 61643-11 (protects LED driver from surge events)
  • Dedicated MCB sized at 1.25× LED rated current (per IEC 60898-1)
  • RCBO Type AC minimum (Type A for kitchen + bathroom areas)
  • Driver IP rating matching installation environment (IP65+ for outdoor / wet area)

Pakistani 2026 LED installation rules

  1. Match LED CCT to room function (warm bedrooms, cool workshops)
  2. Specify lm output, not just W rating — use lm/W as the value metric
  3. Demand IEC + IES certification numbers for batch orders (lighting projects)
  4. Install surge protection on outdoor / commercial / industrial LED feeders
  5. Bond LED metal housings to earth per IEC 60364
  6. Avoid mixing CCT in the same room (looks unprofessional in photos)
  7. Plan for driver replacement: drivers fail before LEDs — choose easily-replaceable models

Six mistakes Pakistani buyers make

  1. Buying by W alone — ignoring lm/W efficiency
  2. Tier 5 unbranded LED with no IEC cert (fails inspection, voids fire insurance)
  3. Wrong CCT for room function (cool white in bedroom = sleep disruption)
  4. Skipping surge protection on outdoor LED (LED driver dies in monsoon)
  5. No earth bond on metal LED housings
  6. Buying low-CRI LED for retail / fashion (clothes look wrong)
