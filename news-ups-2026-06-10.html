IEC 62040 classification decoded (VFI / VI / VFD)

IEC 62040-3 classifies UPS by output behavior under three grid conditions: voltage swing, frequency swing, and waveform distortion. The 3-letter code is what to ask for on the spec sheet:

  • VFI (Voltage and Frequency Independent) — Class 1, online double-conversion. Output independent of input across the full operating range.
  • VI (Voltage Independent) — Class 2, line-interactive with AVR. Output voltage regulated but frequency tracks input.
  • VFD (Voltage and Frequency Dependent) — Class 3, standby/offline. Output tracks input until outage triggers battery cut-in.

2026 shift: lithium battery UPS is replacing SLA

The Pakistani residential + small office UPS market is shifting from sealed lead-acid (SLA) batteries to lithium iron phosphate (LiFePO4 / LFP). Reasons:

  • Cycle life: LFP delivers 4,000-6,000 cycles vs SLA's 300-500 cycles (10-15× longer)
  • Energy density: LFP at 100-150 Wh/kg vs SLA at 30-40 Wh/kg (LFP UPS is 1/3 the weight)
  • Pakistani 2026 LiFePO4 1 kWh price: PKR 22,500 - 38,500 (vs SLA equivalent PKR 9,500-14,500 — cost premium pays back in cycle life)
  • NEPRA 2026 net-metering battery storage: LFP is the dominant chemistry approved on prosumer applications
  • Heat tolerance: LFP works 0-55°C vs SLA's 5-25°C optimal — survives Pakistani 45°C+ summers without derating

UPS sizing — the Pakistani buyer rule of thumb

  1. Add up all loads in VA: each device's wattage divided by power factor 0.8 = VA needed
  2. Add 25% safety margin for surge currents
  3. Round up to next standard size
  4. Decide topology based on load type

Example: Home office with 2 PCs (300W each), 2 LED monitors (35W each), router (15W), inkjet printer (45W). Total: 730W / 0.8 = 912 VA. Add 25% safety: 1,140 VA → round up to 1.5 kVA line-interactive UPS = PKR 32,500 (LFP) typical.
