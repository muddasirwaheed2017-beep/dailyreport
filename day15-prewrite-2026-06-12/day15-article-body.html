What "Industrial Audible Signalling" Actually Covers

The word "buzzer" gets used loosely in Pakistan. Inside an electrical panel, the buzzer is a 24 V DC or 230 V AC alarm fed by relay contacts; on a factory gate, the buzzer is a 110 dB siren; at a mosque, the buzzer is an annunciator tied to azaan timing. These are three different products with three different IP ratings, mounting styles, and decibel envelopes.   • Indoor doorbells — residential and office use, 12 V DC or 230 V AC, 60 to 75 dB(A), IP20
  • Panel-mount buzzers — DIN-rail or panel-face mounting, 24 V DC dominant, 70 to 85 dB(A), IP40 to IP54
  • Factory / outdoor sirens — 110 to 120 dB(A), 230 V AC, IP54 to IP65, weather-resistant
  • Multi-tone sounders — selectable tones (continuous, intermittent, slow whoop, fast whoop), 100 to 110 dB(A), IP54

How to Choose dB(A) Correctly — The Rule of 10 + Distance

Sound pressure level drops by approximately 6 dB(A) every time the distance from the source doubles (inverse square law in free field). In a factory with reverberation, the drop is closer to 4 dB(A) per doubling. The rule for selecting an industrial sounder is:

  1. Identify the furthest worker from the sounder position (in metres).
  2. Measure or estimate the ambient noise at that worker's position (dB(A)). Typical Pakistani factory floor under load is 78 to 90 dB(A).
  3. The sounder at the worker's ear must read at least 10 dB(A) above ambient for clear discrimination — 15 dB(A) above ambient for an evacuation signal per ISO 7731.
  4. Working backwards from the rated 1 m output, subtract 4 dB(A) per doubling of distance.

Example: A worker is 16 m from the sounder, ambient is 85 dB(A). You need 100 dB(A) at his position. Distance doubling from 1 m to 16 m is four doublings (1 → 2 → 4 → 8 → 16) = 16 dB(A) drop in a reverberant factory. Required sounder rating at 1 m: 100 + 16 = 116 dB(A) minimum. So a 120 dB(A) factory siren is correct; a 100 dB(A) panel buzzer is dangerously inadequate.

Voltage Selection — 24 V DC versus 230 V AC

The voltage choice is determined by what drives the sounder, not what is convenient at the install location.

  • 24 V DC — driven by PLC output, control relay, fire-alarm panel, BMS. Most modern industrial signalling is 24 V DC. Failure-safe, low touch-voltage, intrinsically safer in dusty environments.
  • 230 V AC — driven by mains relay, manual switch, simple timer. Acceptable for fixed installations where no PLC or BMS is in the loop. Avoid in damp or dusty environments where IP rating may be compromised.
  • 12 V DC — driven by alarm panel battery, vehicle battery, small UPS. Lowest output range — only for short-distance signalling.

Wiring & Cable Selection

Sounders are usually under-specified in cable size, which causes voltage drop and audible weakness. Use:

  • For 24 V DC sounder up to 50 m run: 1.5 mm² Cu twin-twisted
  • For 24 V DC sounder 50 to 150 m run: 2.5 mm² Cu twin-twisted, shielded if near VFD or contactor
  • For 230 V AC sounder: 1.5 mm² Cu twin-and-earth, run separately from data and signal cables to avoid induced noise
  • Always include an earth conductor for IP54+ metal-bodied sounders. Bond to the panel PE bar at the MET.

Mounting & Orientation

  • Wall-mount sirens face downward at 15° to 30° below horizontal — pushes sound across the working plane, avoids resonance peaks at ceiling level.
  • Beacon-sounders mount with the LED dome facing the line-of-sight of the workers, not the wall.
  • Outdoor mosque sirens point along the prayer-call sweep arc — usually parallel to the qibla wall.
  • School period bells distribute one zone-bell per 1,200 sq ft of corridor with line-of-sight overlap.

5 Most Expensive Mistakes Pakistani Installers Make

  1. Using IP20 indoor doorbell on an outdoor wall. The first monsoon kills the diaphragm. Replace with IP54 outdoor-rated chime.
  2. Specifying dB(A) by guess. A 90 dB(A) sounder in a 95 dB(A) ambient factory is inaudible. Use the distance-and-ambient calculation above.
  3. 230 V AC sounder fed from PLC relay. PLC relays are typically rated 5 A at 230 V with arc suppression for resistive loads only. An inductive AC sounder draws 1.2 to 2 A inrush and welds the relay contact in months. Use a 24 V DC sounder with the PLC relay, or interpose an MCB-rated control relay for 230 V AC sounders.
  4. No surge protection on outdoor sirens. A 110 dB(A) outdoor siren at the top of a minaret is the lightning attractor of the building. Install a Type 2 SPD on the supply to the siren circuit, bonded to the building lightning protection earth.
  5. Mixing sounder cable with 11 kV bus duct or VFD output. Capacitive crosstalk induces 50 Hz hum or PWM whine into the audible loop, making the sounder buzz continuously. Run the sounder cable in its own metal conduit, or in a segregated tray section ≥ 200 mm from power conductors.

FAQ — Industrial Doorbell, Buzzer & Siren Pakistan 2026

What dB(A) sounder do I need for a 5,000 sq ft factory in Lahore?

For a 5,000 sq ft factory with workers up to 22 m from the sounder, allowing 8 to 10 dB(A) reverberation drop per doubling and 80 to 90 dB(A) ambient, you need a sounder rated 110 to 116 dB(A) at 1 m. Single-tone industrial siren or multi-tone electronic sounder both work; multi-tone is preferred for fire versus evacuation discrimination.

Can I use a residential doorbell at a small office?

Yes for an office without machinery and indoor ambient under 55 dB(A). Choose a 75 dB(A) chime rated IP20, mounted indoors on a wall away from the entry door. For office outdoor walls or gates, step up to IP44 wireless or IP54 panel-mount.

What is the difference between a panel-mount buzzer and an industrial siren?

Panel-mount buzzers are 22 mm or 30 mm pilot devices designed to indicate a panel-level alarm (motor overload, low oil pressure, supply loss) at 70 to 85 dB(A) close range. Industrial sirens are 110 to 120 dB(A) full-volume devices designed for area-wide signalling (shift change, fire, evacuation). The two are not interchangeable; using a buzzer where a siren is needed leaves the worker uninformed and creates legal exposure under occupational safety rules.

How long does a Pakistani industrial siren last in coastal Karachi?

An IP54 motor-driven mechanical siren typically gives 4 to 6 years in coastal Karachi before salt-corrosion of the rotor bearings. IP65 electronic sounders with epoxy-sealed diaphragms typically give 8 to 12 years in the same environment. The PKR premium for IP65 over IP54 is 20 to 35 percent and pays back inside one replacement cycle.

Do I need surge protection on a mosque azaan sounder mounted on a minaret?

Yes. A 12 m or higher minaret with a mounted sounder is a lightning attractor. The sounder supply circuit needs a Type 2 SPD per IEC 61643-11 at the panel feeding the minaret, with the SPD's earth conductor bonded to the building's lightning protection earth termination per IEC 62305-3. Without this, one direct or nearby strike destroys the sounder, the controller, and often the supply circuit cabling.

Can I drive a 230 V AC siren from a PLC output relay?

Not directly. PLC output relays are typically rated 5 A at 230 V for resistive loads with limited inductive switching capacity. An AC siren draws 1.2 to 2 A inrush at start and contains motor or transformer windings, which weld the relay contacts within months. The correct approach is either (a) a 24 V DC siren with the PLC relay, or (b) interpose a contactor or interface relay sized for the AC load between the PLC output and the siren coil.

What is the right cable size for a 24 V DC sounder 80 m from the panel?

Voltage drop is the dominant constraint at 24 V DC. For an 80 m run at 24 V DC drawing 0.5 A, using 1.5 mm² copper gives 1.4 V drop (5.8 percent — borderline). Step up to 2.5 mm² copper to give a 0.8 V drop (3.3 percent — safe margin) and add shielding if the run passes near a VFD output cable or contactor coil.

Is there a single sounder that does both door-chime indoors and siren outdoors?

No. Door-chimes (60 to 72 dB(A)) and outdoor sirens (105 to 120 dB(A)) span a 50 dB(A) range and are designed to different IP and lifecycle standards. Specifying the right product per location is more cost-effective and reliable than seeking one dual-purpose device.
