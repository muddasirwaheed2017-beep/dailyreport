3. Why C-curve (not B-curve)

AC compressor inrush current is 5-7× the running current for 1-3 seconds at startup. A B-curve MCB (trips at 3-5×) will nuisance-trip every time the AC kicks on. C-curve (5-10×) handles this cleanly while still protecting against actual overload or short-circuit.

Same rule for fridges, motors, and pumps — C-curve handles inrush. B-curve is for pure resistive loads (heaters, lighting).

4. Wiring sequence for a 3-ton AC dedicated circuit

  1. Run a dedicated 4 mm² copper feed from your main DB box to the AC location.
  2. In the DB box: MCB 20A C-curve in series with RCBO 30mA Type A.
  3. Optionally: voltage protector upstream of MCB (cuts power on voltage events).
  4. Optionally: AC SPD Type II at DB-box main, not on the AC circuit specifically.
  5. Terminate at the AC outdoor unit junction box.
  6. Earth: bond outdoor unit chassis to building earth electrode.

6. FAQ

What's the best AC breaker for a 3-ton air conditioner in Pakistan?

CNC YCB7 1P 20A C-curve MCB (PKR 500) paired with YCB10 RCBO 20A Type A (PKR 4,800) and YC7VA 32A voltage protector (PKR 3,400). Total: ~PKR 8,700 for core protection, ~PKR 11,200 for full bundle including SPD.

Can I use a B-curve breaker for an AC?

Not recommended — B-curve will nuisance-trip on compressor startup. Always C-curve for ACs, fridges, motors, pumps.

Do I need an RCBO or just an MCB?

NEPRA + PEC code requires earth-leakage protection on socket circuits. RCBO combines MCB + RCCB functionality. For AC circuits feeding through a wall socket, RCBO is mandatory.

Will a 16A MCB work for 3-ton AC?

13-15A continuous FLC is right at 16A MCB's limit. Best practice: 20A MCB for safe margin and to handle Pakistani voltage sag (motor draws more current at lower voltage). 16A is risky.

Cheapest CNC 3-ton AC bundle?

MCB only: PKR 500. MCB + voltage protector: PKR 3,900. Full bundle: PKR 11,200.
