"Breaker switch" is among the most-confused electrical terms among Pakistani buyers in 2026 — an estimated 900 monthly searches mix three different products: MCB (miniature circuit breaker), main switch (isolator), and combined RCBO. Get the wrong one and you either lose overcurrent protection or earth-leakage protection. This news item explains the difference, lists current Pakistani 2026 prices, and flags what NEPRA 2026 net-metering installation actually requires.

When to ask for each in Pakistani buying conversation

  • "Bring me a breaker for kitchen socket circuit" → you need an MCB (32A typical), PKR 425-985
  • "Bring me the main switch for the new house DB" → you need a main isolator + RCCB combo, NOT a breaker, PKR 1,250-3,500
  • "Bring me a switch for the AC outlet" → you need MCB + voltage protector + maybe RCBO for kitchen/bathroom AC, PKR 1,850-6,500 bundle
  • "NEPRA inspection asked for Type B RCBO" → you need a Type B residual current circuit breaker (the +leakage version), PKR 8,500-18,500 — mandatory for solar systems with battery storage 2026 onward

NEPRA 2026 net-metering breaker rule

Under NEPRA's 2026 prosumer regulations, any net-metered solar installation with battery storage must use a Type B RCBO (residual current breaker with overcurrent) on the DC + AC interface. Type AC (the cheaper version still common in Pakistani residential) detects only AC residual current and misses DC residual leakage from battery + inverter systems. Pakistani installers using Type AC on net-metered + battery systems fail the audit.
