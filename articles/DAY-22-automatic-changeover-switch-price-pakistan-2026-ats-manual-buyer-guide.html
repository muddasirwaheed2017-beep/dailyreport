Automatic & Manual Changeover Switch Price in Pakistan 2026 — ATS/MTS Buyer Guide

Pakistan's load-shedding reality — 6 to 18 hours of WAPDA outages depending on the feeder and season — means a changeover switch is no longer optional for homes, offices, factories, or agricultural setups. The changeover is the legal and safe interface between the grid and your backup source (generator, UPS, or solar inverter). Buy the wrong one and you risk back-feeding into WAPDA lines, burning out motors, or voiding your generator warranty. This guide covers every type available in Pakistan in 2026, with PKR price ranges, sizing rules, installation safety, and a pre-purchase checklist.

How to Size a Changeover Switch — Pakistan Load Calculation

Every changeover is rated in amperes (A). Buying undersized causes overheating and premature failure. Oversizing wastes money but causes no harm. Use this formula:

  1. List your backup loads: write down every appliance you will run on generator/UPS power (not just current loads — plan for 2–3 years ahead).
  2. Sum total wattage: add all wattage values. Include starting surge for motors (induction motors draw 5–7× rated current on start).
  3. Convert to amps: Amps = Total Watts ÷ 220 (single-phase) or ÷ (380 × 1.73) for 3-phase.
  4. Apply 1.25 safety factor: multiply amps × 1.25.
  5. Round up to standard rating: 32A, 63A, 100A, 125A, 160A, 200A, 250A.

Pakistan-Specific Installation Notes

Single-Phase vs 3-Phase Feeder

Most urban Pakistani homes are on single-phase 220V WAPDA supply. If you have a single-phase generator to match, a 2-pole changeover (switching live + neutral) is standard. However, if your area has 3-phase supply even for residential use — or if you plan a 3-phase generator or solar inverter — a 4-pole changeover is mandatory. Switching only 2 poles on a 3-phase supply leaves two phases energised from WAPDA while the generator is running: extreme electrocution hazard.

Neutral Bonding and Back-Feed Protection

Pakistan's most dangerous wiring error: connecting the generator's neutral to WAPDA's neutral without a break-before-make switch. The correct approach:

  • The changeover must provide complete electrical isolation between WAPDA and generator supplies — both mechanically interlocked so both cannot be connected simultaneously.
  • Generator neutral should only bond to the main earth electrode when running isolated from WAPDA (i.e., in island mode).
  • If WAPDA and generator neutral are ever paralleled through the changeover, back-feed current will flow on the neutral — creating fire risk and electrocuting WAPDA linemen working on the "dead" feeder.
  • A licensed electrician must verify the neutral bonding arrangement before energising any generator installation.

ATS Time Delay Settings for UPS + Generator Combination

If you have both a UPS and a generator, set your ATS transfer delay to at least 30 seconds after WAPDA failure. This allows the UPS to bridge the outage while the generator starts and reaches stable voltage/frequency. If the ATS transfers immediately (2-second delay), the generator's cold start voltage fluctuation can damage sensitive electronics and shorten UPS battery life. Most quality ATS units have an adjustable delay from 5 to 120 seconds — set it to 30–45 seconds for a typical Pakistani home setup.

Outdoor and Meter-Room Installations

Pakitan meter rooms are often semi-outdoor, dusty, and subject to monsoon humidity. Specify a changeover with enclosure rating of at least IP54 (dust-protected, splash-resistant). For fully outdoor generator kiosks, use IP65 or better. Enclosure material should be UV-stabilised polycarbonate or powder-coated steel. Avoid bare metal enclosures without paint — the combination of humidity and dust accelerates corrosion on contact terminals.

5 Expensive Mistakes Pakistani Buyers Make with Changeover Switches

  1. Undersizing the current rating. Buying a 32A changeover for a house that draws 28A at peak means the unit runs near thermal limit constantly — contacts degrade within 12–18 months. Always size up to the next standard rating and include the 1.25× safety factor.
  2. Ignoring the pole count for 3-phase. Installing a 2-pole changeover on a 3-phase supply leaves two phases permanently connected to WAPDA while the generator runs on the third. This is not a wiring error — it is a safety emergency. Always use 4-pole for any 3-phase supply.
  3. Setting ATS transfer delay to zero with a UPS in the system. Instant ATS transfer exposes the UPS to generator voltage and frequency transients during cold start, degrading batteries and inverter components. Set minimum 30-second delay.
  4. No surge protection on the output side. Generators in Pakistan (especially petrol units) produce voltage spikes on start-up and shutdown. A voltage protector or surge protection device on the load side of the changeover protects appliances from these transients and is strongly recommended.
  5. Using a changeover without a matching MCB on each source. The changeover switches the source but does not protect the downstream circuit from faults on either supply. Each incoming source (WAPDA feed, generator output) must have a properly rated MCB or MCCB upstream of the changeover input terminals.

Changeover Switch vs Transfer Switch — Terminology in Pakistan

In the Pakistani electrical market the terms are used interchangeably:

  • Changeover switch / change over switch — the common local term for any manual or automatic source-switching device.
  • ATS (Automatic Transfer Switch) — specifically for automatic switching, often used in commercial/industrial contexts.
  • Transfer switch / bypass switch — used in UPS and data-centre contexts where maintenance bypass is also required.
  • Inverter changeover — specifically for solar hybrid inverter to WAPDA changeover; some hybrid solar inverters have a built-in changeover relay and do not need an external ATS.

10-Point Pre-Purchase Checklist

  1. Calculate your total backup load in watts and convert to amps — size the changeover at 1.25× the calculated value.
  2. Confirm single-phase or 3-phase supply — buy 2-pole for single-phase, 4-pole for 3-phase (no exceptions).
  3. Decide: manual (MTS) or automatic (ATS) based on whether your generator is manual or auto-start.
  4. Verify IEC 60947-3 (manual) or IEC 60947-6-1 (automatic) compliance — ask for datasheet.
  5. Check breaking capacity: minimum 6 kA Ics for residential, 10 kA for commercial.
  6. Confirm mechanical interlock is present — both sources must be physically impossible to connect simultaneously.
  7. For ATS with UPS: confirm adjustable transfer delay (set to 30–45 seconds minimum).
  8. Check enclosure IP rating: IP54 minimum for meter rooms, IP65 for outdoor.
  9. Verify neutral switching: 4-pole units switch neutral — verify this is appropriate for your neutral bonding arrangement.
  10. Buy a voltage protector for the load side and ensure MCBs are rated on both source terminals before installation.

For Tubewell and Agricultural Installations

Tubewell motors in Pakistan are predominantly 3-phase induction motors (7.5 HP to 30 HP). For agricultural changeover setups, specify a 4-pole ATS with a minimum 25A per phase for a 7.5 HP motor, scaling up to 80A per phase for 30 HP. Include a phase failure relay (PFR) downstream of the changeover to cut power if WAPDA or generator phase is lost — protecting the motor from single-phasing which burns windings within minutes. Summary

The right changeover switch for Pakistan in 2026 is sized at 1.25× your backup load current, correctly poled for your supply type (2-pole single-phase, 4-pole three-phase), IEC-certified with ≥6 kA breaking capacity, and installed with proper neutral bonding to prevent back-feed into WAPDA. Automatic ATS units are the correct choice for any setup with an auto-start generator or frequent night-time outages. Budget Rs 8,000–35,000 for a quality single-phase ATS at 32A–63A, and Rs 35,000–80,000 for a 3-phase unit. Pair it with voltage protection on the load side and properly rated MCBs on each source terminal.
