What is a Thermal Overload Relay and How Does It Work?

A thermal overload relay is a protective device connected in series with a motor's power supply — typically inside a motor control panel, mounted directly onto or alongside a magnetic contactor. It continuously monitors current flowing to the motor and trips (opens its contact) if that current exceeds the set threshold for long enough to indicate a real overload condition rather than a normal starting surge.

The bimetal principle: Three bimetallic strips — one per phase — are heated by the motor current passing through heater elements wound around them. As current rises, strips heat and bend. When current exceeds the FLC setting for the thermal trip time defined by the relay's trip class, the strips have bent far enough to release a spring-loaded tripping mechanism, opening the relay's normally-closed (NC) contact in the control circuit. This de-energises the contactor coil, which opens the main power contacts and stops the motor.

The electronic principle: Current transformers (CTs) on each phase feed a microprocessor that calculates true RMS current and applies a thermal model algorithm. The processor monitors not just magnitude but also thermal history — a motor that ran at 110% FLC for 30 minutes accumulates heat that a fresh-start bimetal would not detect. This gives electronic relays superior protection for motors with frequent starts, ambient temperature changes, or intermittent duty cycles.

Types of Thermal Overload Relays Available in Pakistan

Bimetal Thermal Overload Relay

The most common type in Pakistan. Three bimetallic heater elements, manual or automatic reset, adjustable FLC dial, phase-loss sensitivity (Class F and above). Sizes range from 0.1A to 800A. Suitable for: general factory motors, agricultural pump starters, HVAC fans, conveyor drives. Price range: Rs 450–2,800.

Electronic Thermal Overload Relay

Microprocessor-based, CT input, digital display of current on premium models, true RMS measurement, phase-loss detection, phase imbalance detection (usually 20–40% threshold), ground fault detection (optional module), remote reset via auxiliary contact. Suitable for: critical motors (compressors, chillers, expensive submersible pumps), motors in hazardous areas where manual reset is dangerous, motors with variable duty cycles. Price range: Rs 2,500–8,500.

Solid State Overload Relay

No moving parts, semiconductor-based thermal model, very fast response, immune to vibration and dust, extended temperature operation range. Used in severe environments: cement plant conveyor rooms, agricultural equipment exposed to husk/dust. Less common in Pakistani market. Price range: Rs 4,000–12,000.

Manual Motor Starter (MMS) with Built-In Overload

Combined MCCB + overload + manual on/off in one compact unit. Rated up to 32A (approximately 15 HP at 380V 3-phase). Popular in Pakistani panel boards for smaller motors where a separate contactor + overload combination is oversized. Price range: Rs 1,800–6,500.

FLC Setting Dial — How to Set Your Thermal Overload Relay

Every thermal overload relay has an adjustable dial marked with current values between the relay's minimum and maximum range. Setting this dial correctly is the single most important adjustment in motor protection.

Step-by-step FLC setting procedure used by Pakistani panel builders:

  1. Find motor nameplate FLC: Read the motor nameplate for "Amps at 380V" (3-phase) or "Amps at 220V" (single-phase). This is your target FLC.
  2. Verify with clamp meter (best practice): Run the motor at full mechanical load and measure actual current on all three phases with a clamp meter. Use the highest reading as your FLC. If all three phases are not within 5% of each other, investigate for phase imbalance before setting the relay.
  3. Set dial to measured FLC: Turn the relay adjustment dial to match the measured FLC. Most Pakistani bimetal relays have a dial numbered in amperes and show a mark or pointer at the setting.
  4. Apply WAPDA compensation: If your WAPDA supply is known to run below 375V, add 5% to the dial setting. Example: measured FLC = 8.0A → dial to 8.4A.
  5. Do NOT set above 115% of nameplate FLC: Setting the relay above 115% of motor nameplate FLC provides no meaningful protection — the motor can overheat before the relay trips.

Common mistake in Pakistan: Many panel builders set the relay to its maximum range end (e.g., setting a 7–10A relay to 10A on a 5 HP / 8.5A motor) assuming "higher setting = more reliable." This is wrong — it means the relay will not trip until current exceeds 10A, allowing the motor to run at 115% FLC without protection for extended periods.

Contactor + Thermal Overload Relay Motor Starter — The Standard Pakistani Panel Configuration

A thermal overload relay does not switch power directly — it monitors current and provides a control signal to the contactor via its normally-closed (NC) contact in the control circuit. The standard motor starter panel configuration in Pakistan consists of:

  1. MCCB or fuse-switch unit — short-circuit protection (tens of milliseconds)
  2. Magnetic contactor — main switching device, energised by control circuit
  3. Thermal overload relay — mounted on contactor, NC contact in series with contactor coil
  4. Start/Stop pushbuttons — control circuit, connected through overload relay NC contact

When the overload relay trips, its NC contact opens, breaking the contactor coil circuit. The contactor de-energises and drops out, opening the main power contacts. The motor stops. The overload relay must be manually reset (by pressing the reset button on the relay) before the motor can restart. This latching behaviour prevents automatic restart after a thermal trip — an important safety feature in Pakistan where unattended motor restarts have caused industrial accidents.

Pakistan-Specific Application Scenarios

Agricultural Tubewell Motors (Most Common)

Most Pakistani tubewell motors are 2–10 HP single or 3-phase DOL-started. Use Trip Class 20 bimetal relay. Set FLC 5% above nameplate for WAPDA voltage compensation. Replace relay if contactor is replaced — old relay heater elements calibrate to original contactor's thermal characteristics. Common failure mode in Pakistan: relay trips in summer afternoon (WAPDA at 350V, motor draws higher current + ambient temperature above 45°C reduces relay trip threshold). Fix: shade the control panel and use Class 20 instead of Class 10.

Factory Conveyor Motors (Frequent Pakistan Failure)

Conveyors start under load in Pakistan (material already on belt at start). This causes higher inrush. Use Class 20 or Class 30 depending on belt length and material weight. Do not use Class 10 — will trip on loaded start. If tripping persists, verify belt tension and verify contactor is actually achieving DOL start (loose connection in starter can cause single-phase start which doubles current on remaining two phases).

HVAC Compressors

Compressor motors have capacitive phase-shift starting (single-phase) or across-the-line starting (3-phase). For commercial split AC and chiller compressors in Pakistan, use electronic overload relay with phase imbalance detection — compressor runs expensive liquid refrigerant load and single-phase operation caused by a blown fuse will destroy the compressor within minutes. Electronic relay detects phase imbalance within 3–5 seconds and trips before damage.

Submersible Pump Motors (Deep Bore)

Most critical application in Pakistan — if a submersible burns at 300 feet, motor replacement requires pump extraction crew, Rs 15,000–80,000 in labour and pump service costs. Invest in an electronic overload relay with phase loss and thermal history protection. Set relay for automatic lockout (no auto-reset) so that a motor that tripped while unattended does not automatically restart on WAPDA restoration and burn in a second overload event.

Single-Phase Motor Protection (Pakistan Domestic & Agricultural)

Single-phase motors (0.5–3 HP, 220V) are not protected by standard 3-phase thermal overload relays. Use: (a) single-phase electronic overload relay, or (b) a current-sensing relay combined with a timer relay for DOL start bypass. For domestic water pumps in Pakistan, a simple motor protection relay (under-voltage + over-current + dry-run detection) available from Rs 800–2,500 provides combined protection that a thermal relay alone cannot.

Frequently Asked Questions — Thermal Overload Relay Pakistan 2026

What is the price of a thermal overload relay in Pakistan in 2026?

Thermal overload relay prices in Pakistan 2026 range from Rs 450 for a basic bimetal relay (0.1–0.16A range) to Rs 8,500 for an advanced electronic overload relay with ground fault protection. Most common 1–32A bimetal relays cost Rs 900–2,800 depending on the ampere range and brand.

How do I select a thermal overload relay for my motor in Pakistan?

Match the relay's current range to your motor's Full Load Current (FLC). For a 5 HP (3.7 kW) motor on 380V 3-phase, FLC is approximately 8.5A — select a relay with a 6–10A or 7–10A range and set the dial to 8.5A. In Pakistan, add 5–10% upward compensation for WAPDA under-voltage conditions (typical 360–370V supply vs. rated 380V).

What is the difference between a bimetal and electronic thermal overload relay?

A bimetal thermal overload relay uses temperature-sensitive bi-metallic strips that bend when heated by motor current, tripping a mechanical contact. It is simple, reliable, and low-cost (Rs 450–2,800). An electronic overload relay uses current transformers and microprocessor logic to measure true RMS current and calculate thermal model — it provides precise protection, phase-loss detection, ground fault detection, and remote reset, but costs Rs 2,500–8,500.

What trip class should I use for a tubewell motor in Pakistan?

Use Trip Class 20 (CLASS 20) for submersible tubewell motors in Pakistan. Class 20 allows up to 20 seconds at 7.2× FLC before tripping, accommodating the high starting current of submersible pumps without nuisance tripping. Class 10 is too sensitive and will trip during normal DOL starting of deep-bore pumps.

Can I use a thermal overload relay with a single-phase motor in Pakistan?

Yes, but use a single-phase thermal overload relay rated for single-phase operation. Most bimetal relays sold in Pakistan are 3-phase units — connecting a single-phase motor through two of the three poles without disabling the phase-loss detection circuit will cause nuisance tripping. A dedicated single-phase overload relay or an electronic relay with single-phase mode prevents this.

How do I reset a thermal overload relay after a trip in Pakistan?

Wait 2–3 minutes for the bimetal strips to cool before pressing the manual reset button. If the relay trips again immediately on reset, do not repeatedly reset — this indicates a real overload condition: check motor current draw with a clamp meter, inspect motor winding resistance, and verify the relay FLC setting. Repeated forced resets without diagnosis can destroy the motor winding.
