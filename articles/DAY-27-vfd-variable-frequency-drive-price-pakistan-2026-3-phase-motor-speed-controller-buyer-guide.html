What Is a Variable Frequency Drive (VFD)?

A variable frequency drive — also called an inverter drive, AC drive, or frequency converter — controls the speed and torque of a 3-phase AC induction motor by changing the frequency and voltage of the power supply it delivers to the motor. Inside the drive, a rectifier converts AC to DC, a DC bus stores energy in capacitors, and an IGBT inverter regenerates AC at whatever frequency the controller commands.

The result: a 1,450 RPM motor running at 50 Hz baseline can be slowed to 725 RPM at 25 Hz, or accelerated to 1,740 RPM at 60 Hz. Speed follows frequency linearly; torque is maintained by keeping V/f ratio constant in standard V/f mode.

VFD vs Inverter — Pakistan Terminology Note

In Pakistani electrical markets the word "inverter" is used for two completely different products: solar power inverters (DC to AC for household use) and AC motor drives (VFDs). This guide covers only motor VFDs. For solar power inverters, see our hybrid inverter price guide.

Why Pakistani Engineers Are Specifying VFDs in 2026

Five drivers are accelerating VFD adoption in Pakistan:

  1. Energy cost crisis. WAPDA commercial tariff reached Rs 65–72/kWh in 2026. A centrifugal pump running at 80% speed uses only 51% of full-speed power (affinity laws: P ∝ n³). Payback on a VFD is typically 6–18 months in industrial pump applications.
  2. Single-phase supply shortage. Tubewell feeders in Punjab and Sindh are frequently single-phase. Single-phase-input VFDs (220V in, 3×220V out) allow a 3-phase motor to run from a single-phase line — a critical capability where three-phase supply is unavailable.
  3. WAPDA power quality. Voltage fluctuations, phase imbalance, and momentary dips damage motors started DOL. VFDs with input chokes and phase-loss protection absorb these variations.
  4. Solar-VFD hybrid systems. Combined with MPPT solar input or a solar inverter DC bus, VFDs allow daytime agricultural pump operation with zero grid cost. See our solar pump controller guide for DC VFD variants.
  5. Motor soft-start eliminating starter panels. A VFD accelerates smoothly over 5–30 seconds, eliminating the need for a separate star-delta or DOL starter panel — saving panel cost and reducing peak current penalties on WAPDA commercial connections.

How to Size a VFD — The 4-Step Method for Pakistan

Step 1: Motor Nameplate kW (not HP)

Convert HP → kW: multiply HP × 0.746. Examples:

  • 5 HP × 0.746 = 3.73 kW → buy 3.7 or 4 kW VFD
  • 10 HP × 0.746 = 7.46 kW → buy 7.5 kW VFD
  • 20 HP × 0.746 = 14.9 kW → buy 15 kW VFD
  • 50 HP × 0.746 = 37.3 kW → buy 37 kW VFD

Always match kW, not HP. HP ratings on Pakistani motors are often rounded; always read the kW figure from the nameplate.

Step 2: Check Full-Load Amps (FLA)

The VFD's output current rating (Amps) must exceed the motor's nameplate full-load amps. For a 7.5 kW / 380V motor: FLA ≈ 16.5A. The VFD must be rated ≥ 16.5A continuous output. This matters for high-efficiency motors that may draw slightly different current than their kW rating suggests.

Step 3: Apply the Service Factor (SF)

For high-inertia loads (centrifugal pumps, fans) SF = 1.0–1.1. For variable-torque loads with shock (crushers, conveyor with full load start) SF = 1.15–1.25. Multiply motor kW × SF to get minimum VFD kW rating.

Step 4: Single-Phase Derating

If running from single-phase supply: motor kW ÷ 0.65 = minimum VFD nameplate kW. A 3.7 kW motor needs ≥ 5.7 kW → buy a 7.5 kW drive.

Single-Phase Input VFDs — The Pakistani Tubewell Solution

Single-phase-to-three-phase VFDs are among the most sold drive types in Pakistan's agricultural and semi-urban markets. The reason: rural WAPDA feeders frequently supply single-phase (220V) only, or the three-phase supply is so unbalanced that 3-phase motors connected DOL burn out within months.

How Single-Phase Input VFDs Work

The drive rectifies 220V single-phase into a 310V DC bus. A 6-pulse IGBT inverter then synthesizes balanced three-phase 220V output at the commanded frequency. The limitation is DC bus current: a single-phase rectifier can only deliver ~60–70% of the rated DC bus current compared to a three-phase rectifier. Hence the derating.

Parameter Settings for Single-Phase Input Operation

  • Input mode: Set to "single-phase input" if the drive has this parameter (e.g., Huanyang: P00.18 = 1; typical default = 0 for 3P)
  • Acceleration time: Extend to 15–30 seconds minimum to reduce DC bus stress
  • Overcurrent level: Set at 130% (not 150%) of motor rated current when single-phase derated
  • Input voltage lower limit: Set at 190V to trip early on voltage sag rather than blow IGBTs

VFD for Water Pump Applications in Pakistan

Pump-drive applications are the single largest VFD market segment in Pakistan. Key considerations:

Pressure Control with a VFD (PID Mode)

Connect a pressure transducer (4–20 mA) to the VFD's analog input. Set the PID target pressure (e.g., 3 bar). The drive adjusts motor speed automatically to maintain constant pressure — eliminating the need for a pressure tank and float switch in many agricultural layouts.

Sleep / Wake Mode

Most pump VFDs support a sleep function: when demand drops below a set threshold (drive at minimum frequency for N seconds), the drive stops the motor. When pressure drops below wake threshold, it restarts. This is critical for intermittent agricultural demand — the motor doesn't run all night at minimum speed wasting energy.

Anti-Water Hammer at Stop

Rapid pump stop causes pressure surges (water hammer) that can split pipes. Set the VFD deceleration time to 10–30 seconds. Enable DC injection braking at stop for positive motor halt without mechanical backflow.

Affinity Law Energy Savings — Pakistan Example

A 10 HP pump running at full speed: ~6.5 kW actual load, 10 hours/day = 65 kWh, Rs 4,225/day at Rs 65/kWh. Reducing to 80% speed: power = 6.5 × 0.8³ = 3.33 kW → 33.3 kWh, Rs 2,165/day. Annual saving: Rs 746,500. VFD cost (10 HP / 7.5 kW): Rs 46,000–68,000. Payback: ~33 days.

VFD Installation Requirements for Pakistan

Input Protection

Install a properly rated MCB or MCCB on the supply side — not a thermal-magnetic overload relay (do not use a motor-type overload relay on VFD input). Consult our MCB price and selection guide for breaker sizing. The MCB should be rated at 150–175% of VFD input amps to allow for capacitor inrush at power-on.

Input Choke (AC Reactor)

Required when: (a) supply transformer is < 10× drive kVA; (b) multiple drives share a bus; (c) harmonic distortion must comply with NEC / IEC 61000. An input choke reduces harmonic current THD from ~80–120% to ~35–40%, protects drive IGBTs from voltage spikes, and extends drive life significantly on Pakistani supply.

Output Filter / dV/dt Filter

For motor cable lengths > 30 m, install a dV/dt filter on the VFD output. Long cables cause reflected voltage spikes that damage motor winding insulation. This is critical for submersible pump installations where cable runs may be 50–200 m.

Grounding

VFD must be grounded per IEC 61800-5-1. Earth the motor frame and VFD chassis separately to a common earth bar — not in series. Poor earthing is the most common cause of nuisance ground fault trips and IGBT failure in Pakistani VFD installations.

Panel Enclosure

In Pakistani outdoor/semi-outdoor installations: minimum IP54 enclosure for the drive panel. The VFD itself should have IP20 (standard) and be inside the IP54 panel. Maintain ≥ 50 mm clearance above and below the drive for airflow. In ambient temperatures above 40°C, derate the drive by 1–2% per °C above 40°C or add forced ventilation.

Cable Sizing

Use the same cable sizing rules as for DOL starters — based on motor FLA plus a 25% margin for harmonic derating. Use properly crimped cable lugs at VFD output terminals; never rely on strand-twisting alone on motor drives (vibration loosens bare strands, creating intermittent connections that destroy IGBTs).

Power Factor and VFDs

VFDs improve the displacement power factor of the motor load to near unity (since the motor sees a controlled sinusoidal voltage). However, VFDs introduce harmonic current distortion, which reduces true power factor at the utility meter. A power factor correction capacitor bank should never be connected on the output side of a VFD (it will damage the IGBT output stage). Install PFC capacitors upstream of the VFD input, with a detuning reactor to avoid resonance with VFD harmonics.

VFD Types Available in Pakistan — What You'll Actually Find

General-Purpose V/f Control Drives

The most commonly available drives in Pakistan's electrical wholesale markets. These use V/f (volts-per-hertz) control — adequate for fans, pumps, conveyors. No encoder feedback required. Suitable for 95% of applications sold in the Pakistani market.

Sensorless Vector Control Drives

Uses motor model algorithms to estimate flux and torque without an encoder. Better speed regulation at low frequencies and better torque response than V/f. Suited for hoists, winding machines, precise conveyor speed matching. 20–40% premium over V/f drives at same kW.

Closed-Loop Vector / Servo Drives

Requires motor shaft encoder. Used in CNC machines, printing presses, high-precision textile machines. Typically imported to order; 50–100% premium. Not commonly stocked in Pakistani wholesale markets.

Solar/DC-Input VFDs

Accepts DC input (from solar panels or battery) instead of AC. See our solar pump controller guide for full coverage of this category.

FAQ — VFD Pakistan

Can I use one VFD to run multiple motors?

Yes, if the motors start/stop together and total motor FLA ≤ VFD output rating. Each motor must have its own overload relay on the output side. Sensorless vector and closed-loop modes are not compatible with multi-motor setups — use V/f mode only.

Do I still need a contactor on the VFD output?

No output contactor should switch while the VFD is running — this causes OC or OV faults and can blow IGBTs. If you need a bypass, use a bypass contactor scheme with interlock (VFD de-energised before bypass closes). Consult our contactor selection guide for bypass relay sizing.

What warranty is standard in Pakistan?

Reputable distributors offer 1-year warranty on Chinese-manufactured general-purpose drives, 2 years on European-origin drives. Warranty typically excludes damage from overvoltage, phase loss, and incorrect installation — all common in Pakistan. Always confirm the warranty covers parts and onsite service, not just the drive return-to-base.

Can a VFD protect my motor from WAPDA phase loss?

Yes. All modern VFDs detect input phase loss within 1–5 seconds and trip to protect the motor from single-phasing overheating. This alone often justifies VFD cost versus adding a separate phase failure relay to a DOL panel.

Is a braking resistor required?

For fans and centrifugal pumps: usually not — these are low-inertia loads that decelerate gently. For high-inertia loads (flywheels, centrifuges, winding machines): braking resistor required to dissipate regenerative energy during fast decel, otherwise OV fault occurs. Budget Rs 3,000–25,000 for braking resistor plus resistor housing depending on drive size.

What is the minimum cable distance between VFD and motor?

No minimum. But for cables < 5 m, reflected wave voltage spikes are less severe — a standard cable is acceptable. For 5–30 m: use inverter-rated (variable-torque-rated) cable with shielding. For > 30 m: add dV/dt output filter. For submersible installations > 50 m: dV/dt filter is mandatory.
