Why Pakistan Panels Run Hotter Than European Specs Assume

Most panel components — VFDs, PLCs, DIN rail PSUs, MCCBs, RCCBs — are tested and rated by European manufacturers at a 40°C maximum ambient. Their derating curves start at 40°C. In Pakistan's summer:

  • Factory floor ambient: 45–50°C — already 5–10°C above the derating start point before the panel door closes.
  • Inside an unventilated panel: 60–75°C — the steel enclosure acts as a solar collector in direct sunlight, and the heat from internal components adds another 15–25°C.
  • Inside a VFD cubicle (no cooling): 75–85°C — VFDs are the largest heat sources in most panels; even at 98% efficiency, a 22kW VFD dissipates ~440W continuously.

At 75°C, most electrolytic capacitors rated for 85°C have a service life of only 1,000–3,000 hours — 6–18 months. At 75°C, DIN rail PSUs rated for "40°C full load" operate at roughly 60–65% capacity before thermal foldback activates. At 80°C, IGBT junction temperatures in VFDs approach the absolute maximum, triggering protection trips.

Adding correct cooling is the highest-ROI maintenance action in any Pakistan panel room.

How to Size a Panel Fan for Pakistan — Step-by-Step

Step 1: Calculate total panel heat dissipation

Add the heat generated by every component running at full load:

  • VFD 22kW (98% efficiency): 22,000 × 0.02 = 440W
  • Soft starter 22kW (bypass after start): ~15W during run
  • DIN rail PSU 10A 24VDC: ~15W (see our 24VDC PSU guide)
  • Contactor coil × 3 (220VAC): ~6W each = 18W (see our Contactor guide)
  • MCCB 250A at 80% load: ~20W
  • MCBs, terminal blocks, wiring: ~10W
  • Total in example: ~520W

Step 2: Calculate required airflow

Q (m³/h) = 3.1 × P_heat (W) / ΔT (°C)

Pakistan target ΔT for VFD panel: 10°C (panel interior = ambient + 10°C)
Pakistan target ΔT for PLC/control panel: 15°C

Example (520W, ΔT=10°C):
Q = 3.1 × 520 / 10 = 161 m³/h minimum

Add 30% headroom for filter pressure drop: 161 × 1.3 = 210 m³/h rated airflow required

Step 3: Select fan size

A 230mm fan rated at 280–350 m³/h covers the 210 m³/h requirement with adequate headroom. For smaller panels with 200–300W heat load, a 150mm fan (140–180 m³/h) is sufficient.

Step 4: Set thermostat for Pakistan

  • ON setpoint: 35°C (not 40°C as European manuals suggest)
  • OFF setpoint: 30°C (5°C hysteresis — prevents rapid cycling)
  • Alarm setpoint: 55°C — PLC alarm input or panel warning lamp

Component Derating vs. Temperature — Pakistan Panel Context

Linking cooling requirements back to the components in your panel:

  • VFD / Variable Frequency Drive — IGBT modules derate starting at 40°C ambient. At 50°C ambient inside the panel, most VFDs require 20–30% output current derating. At 60°C, protection trips activate. See our VFD price guide for derating tables by frame size.
  • DIN Rail 24VDC PSU — 70% load derating rule for Pakistan summer panel interiors. Thermal foldback activates at 75°C case temperature. See our 24VDC PSU guide for full sizing methodology.
  • Thermal overload relay — Bimetal elements compensate for ambient up to 40°C. Above 40°C, the relay trips at lower motor current than its dial setting because the bimetal is already warm. Pakistan summer panels need ambient compensation: at 50°C ambient, reduce the overload relay dial setting by 10–15% to avoid nuisance tripping, OR set it 15–20% higher than motor nameplate FLA if overcurrent protection is the priority. See our Thermal Overload Relay guide for compensation tables.
  • Contactor / coil — Class F/H coil insulation — rated to 155–180°C coil temperature. However, the magnetic pull-in force of the coil varies with supply voltage and temperature. In summer WAPDA undervoltage + high ambient, contactor dropout voltage may cause relay to chatter or fail to seal. See our Contactor guide for voltage/temperature operating window.

Anti-Condensation Heater — Pakistan Monsoon & Night-Cooling

Pakistan's monsoon season (July–September) and the sharp nighttime temperature drop in winter create a condensation risk in outdoor and semi-outdoor panels. When a warm panel shuts down and the ambient drops 15–20°C overnight, moisture condenses on PCBs, terminal blocks, and busbar surfaces — causing:

  • Tracking faults on PCBs (carbon track → short circuit)
  • Corrosion on copper terminal connections → increased resistance → hot joint
  • Phase-to-phase tracking on exposed busbar at 400VAC

The solution: a DIN rail anti-condensation heater (100W typically) controlled by an anti-condensation thermostat set to ON below 10°C. Heaters and thermostats for outdoor panels in Pakistan: Rs. 2,200–4,500 for the pair. This is almost always worth fitting on any outdoor panel in Pakistan.

Pakistan IP Rating Rules for Panel Cooling

  • IP42 — Indoor, controlled environment only (panel rooms, air-conditioned electrical rooms). Do NOT use in factory floors with any dust.
  • IP54 — Minimum for indoor factory floor panels. Protects against dust and water splash from any direction. Correct for 70% of Pakistan industrial panels.
  • IP65 — Required for outdoor panels, pump control panels exposed to rain, and any panel in monsoon-exposed locations. Also appropriate for textile mills with heavy flying fibre.
  • Note: The fan IP rating and the panel enclosure IP rating interact. Cutting a hole in an IP65 enclosure for an IP54 fan degrades the whole assembly. Use an IP65-rated fan + filter unit (with its own sealing gasket) in any IP65 application.

7 Costly Panel Cooling Mistakes in Pakistan (With PKR Estimates)

  1. IP42 fan on outdoor panel — Motor winding fails in 1 monsoon season. Replacement fan: Rs. 3,500. Downtime cost (pump panel failure): Rs. 20,000–200,000/event. Always use IP65 outdoors.
  2. Setting thermostat ON at 40°C — European standard, not Pakistan standard. At 40°C ON setting, the panel interior is already at 55–65°C before the fan starts. By then, VFD IGBTs are within minutes of thermal trip. Set ON at 35°C.
  3. Ignoring filter mat replacement — Blocked mat reduces airflow by 80%. Fan motor overheats and fails. Replacement fan: Rs. 5,000–12,000 + motor windings failure diagnostic. In cement plants, blocked mats also draw fine silica dust into the panel on the 20% residual airflow — destroying electronics faster than no filter at all.
  4. Inlet and outlet fans on the same wall of the panel — Correct: inlet fan at the bottom, outlet at the top (hot air rises). Wrong: both fans at the same height on the same face. Short-circuiting the airflow path means the fan stirs air near the fans but the rest of the panel remains hot. Add Rs. 6,000–12,000 to move outlet to top.
  5. Undersized fan — "any fan will do" — A 120mm fan on a 22kW VFD panel is Rs. 2,800 saved and Rs. 30,000–150,000 in VFD repair/replacement plus downtime. Size correctly: Q = 3.1 × P / ΔT + 30% headroom.
  6. No anti-condensation heater on outdoor panel — Pakistan monsoon condensation causes tracking on HV/MV PCBs and terminal blocks. Anti-condensation heater + thermostat: Rs. 2,200–4,500. Phase-to-phase tracking fault on 400VAC busbar: Rs. 50,000–500,000 (replace busbar, panel downtime, motor downtime, production loss).
  7. Fan running 24/7 without thermostat — No thermostat means the fan runs even at 2 AM in January when the panel interior is 5°C. This accelerates bearing wear and consumes power unnecessarily. A thermostat (Rs. 800–2,200) triples the service life of the fan by running it only when needed.

Correct Panel Fan Wiring (230VAC Thermostat-Controlled)

Panel Fan Wiring — Thermostat NC contact → Fan (standard convention)

  L (Phase) ─────┬──────────────────────────────────► (to panel main MCB)
                 │
                 ├──[ Fan MCB 2A ]──[ Thermostat NC contact ]──[ Fan Motor L ]
                 │                                               └── Fan Motor N ──► N (Neutral)
                 │
                 └──[ Anti-cond heater MCB 4A ]──[ Anti-cond thermostat NO contact ]──[ Heater ]──► N

Notes:
- Fan: thermostat NC contact = fan runs when thermostat contact is CLOSED (below setpoint OFF)
  → fan ON above setpoint ON, fan OFF below setpoint OFF (hysteresis)
- Actually: thermostat NO contact: fan energises when temp EXCEEDS setpoint ON
  (Check thermostat datasheet — most panel thermostats use NO contact for cooling)
- Anti-condensation heater: NO contact closes when temp FALLS BELOW setpoint (heating mode)
- Label each MCB: "Panel Fan 1A", "Anti-Cond Heater" — required by NEC/IEC panel labelling standard

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the price of a panel cooling fan in Pakistan?

Panel cooling fans in Pakistan range from Rs. 1,800 to Rs. 12,000 depending on size and IP rating. A standard 120mm DIN rail fan with IP42 costs Rs. 1,800–3,500. A 230mm IP54 thermostat-controlled fan unit costs Rs. 6,000–12,000. Filter mats (spare) cost Rs. 400–1,200 each.

What temperature should I set my panel thermostat in Pakistan summer?

Set the thermostat ON at 35°C with a 5°C OFF hysteresis (OFF at 30°C). European manuals often suggest 40°C ON, but Pakistan summer ambient reaches 45–50°C, so the panel interior reaches 60–70°C without cooling. Setting ON at 35°C provides an early-start margin that prevents thermal runaway in July–August.

How often should I replace panel filter mats in Pakistan?

Pakistan filter mat replacement intervals depend on the industrial environment: textile/cotton mill — every 2–4 weeks; cement/construction — every 1–2 weeks; food processing — every 2–3 weeks; light industrial/office — every 2–3 months. Blocked filter mats are the single biggest cause of panel fan failure in Pakistan.

Do I need a panel air conditioner or can a fan be enough?

In most Pakistan panels with VFDs, PLCs, and DIN rail PSUs, a properly sized fan + filter + thermostat system is sufficient when the ambient temperature is below 45°C and the panel can be placed away from direct sunlight. Panel AC (Rs. 45,000–120,000) is required only when: (a) the panel is in direct outdoor sunlight with no shade, (b) heat dissipation inside the panel exceeds 500W, or (c) the environment is too dusty for any open-frame fan solution.

What IP rating should a panel cooling fan have in Pakistan?

For indoor industrial panels (factory floor): IP54 minimum. For outdoor panels (site switchgear, pump panels): IP65 minimum. IP42 fans are fine only for panel rooms with controlled environments. Never install an IP42 fan on an outdoor panel in Pakistan — monsoon humidity and dust will destroy the motor windings within one season.

What size panel fan do I need for a VFD drive panel?

Calculate the required airflow: Q (m³/h) = 3.1 × P_heat (W) / ΔT (°C), where P_heat is the total heat dissipated in the panel and ΔT is the allowable temperature rise (typically 10–15°C for VFD panels). A 22kW VFD dissipates approximately 440W at 2% loss. Q = 3.1 × 440 / 10 = 136 m³/h minimum. Use a 230mm fan rated 200+ m³/h with 30% headroom for filter resistance.
