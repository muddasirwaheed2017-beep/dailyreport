What Is a Power Factor Capacitor Bank?

A power factor capacitor bank is a bank of capacitors connected in parallel to an AC supply to supply reactive power locally, reducing the reactive-current component drawn from the grid. In simple terms, it is an energy-efficiency device that:

  • Raises your power factor (PF) from a typical 0.70–0.82 to 0.92–0.98
  • Reduces line losses (I²R) in your internal cabling and transformer
  • Eliminates NEPRA reactive-power surcharges
  • Frees up transformer capacity for additional loads
  • Extends motor and transformer life by reducing thermal stress

Units are measured in kVAR (kilovolt-ampere reactive). A capacitor bank that "supplies" 20 kVAR compensates exactly 20 kVAR of lagging reactive load.

Fixed vs. Automatic (APFC) Capacitor Banks

Fixed banks are always connected. Best for loads that run continuously at predictable PF — e.g., a fixed-speed motor running 8 hours/day. Risk of over-compensation at light loads.

Automatic Power Factor Controller (APFC) panels switch capacitor steps in/out automatically via a PF relay. Best for variable industrial loads — factories, workshops, hospitals, commercial buildings. More expensive upfront but eliminates over/under-compensation risk.

How to Size a Capacitor Bank — Step-by-Step

Formula

Qc (kVAR) = P (kW) × (tan φ₁ − tan φ₂)

  • P = active power in kW (from your NEPRA bill or clamp meter)
  • φ₁ = existing power factor angle (e.g., PF 0.75 → tan φ₁ = 0.882)
  • φ₂ = target power factor angle (PF 0.95 → tan φ₂ = 0.329)

Detuning Reactor (Harmonic Filter)

If your load includes variable frequency drives (VFDs), arc furnaces, welding sets, or switched-mode power supplies, the harmonic content in the supply can damage plain capacitors within months. Add a detuned reactor (7% at 189 Hz or 14% at 134 Hz) in series with each capacitor step. This increases unit cost by 25–35% but extends capacitor life from 2 years to 12+ years.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is a power factor capacitor bank?

A power factor capacitor bank is a set of capacitors connected in parallel to your electrical supply to provide reactive power locally. It improves your power factor (PF) from a typical 0.70–0.80 to 0.92–0.98, reducing NEPRA reactive-power surcharges, cutting cable losses, and freeing transformer capacity.

How much does a capacitor bank cost in Pakistan in 2026?

A fixed 10 kVAR capacitor bank costs PKR 14,000–25,000. A 50 kVAR APFC panel (automatic, 6-step) costs PKR 120,000–220,000. Prices depend on IEC certification level, housing material, detuning reactor inclusion, and origin tier (economy / IEC mid-range / premium European-specification).

How do I calculate the kVAR size I need?

Use the formula: Qc (kVAR) = P (kW) × (tan φ₁ − tan φ₂). For a 100 kW factory at 0.75 PF targeting 0.95 PF, you need approximately 55 kVAR. A 50 kVAR fixed bank + 10 kVAR fixed bank = 60 kVAR is a practical choice. For variable loads, choose an APFC panel with auto-switching steps.

What is an APFC panel and do I need one?

An Automatic Power Factor Controller (APFC) panel uses a PF relay to switch capacitor steps in and out automatically as your load varies. If your factory runs different shifts, has variable motor loading, or uses welding/VFD equipment, an APFC panel prevents over-compensation at light load (which causes voltage rise) and under-compensation at peak load. Most industrial consumers above 50 kW benefit from APFC over fixed banks.

What happens if my power factor stays below 0.85 on my NEPRA bill?

NEPRA levies a reactive-power surcharge (typically 2–5 PKR/kVArh excess) on industrial consumers falling below 0.85 monthly average PF. For a 200 kW factory, annual penalties can exceed PKR 300,000–400,000. Some DISCOs (LESCO, MEPCO) also offer incentive credits for maintaining PF ≥ 0.95.

Do I need a detuning reactor with my capacitor bank?

Yes, if your load includes VFDs, welding sets, arc furnaces, UPS systems, or any switched-mode power supply. These create harmonic currents that amplify in capacitors and cause premature failure or resonance. A 7% detuned reactor (189 Hz tuning) blocks 5th-harmonic amplification and is mandatory for modern industrial facilities in Pakistan.

What is the lifespan of a capacitor bank in Pakistani conditions?

Economy-grade capacitors last 4–7 years in Pakistan's hot climate (45–50°C summer ambient). IEC 60831-certified MKP polypropylene units with proper derating last 10–12 years. Premium European-specification units with thermal protection and overpressure disconnectors achieve 15+ years. Always request IEC 60831-1 certification and confirm the unit's rated temperature class (Class D or E preferred for Pakistan).
