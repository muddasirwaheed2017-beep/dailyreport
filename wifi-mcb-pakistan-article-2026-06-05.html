What is a WiFi MCB / WiFi breaker?

A WiFi MCB (also called smart MCB, IoT breaker, WiFi breaker) is a DIN-rail mounted circuit breaker with three integrated functions:

  1. Overcurrent protection — same as a normal MCB (IEC 60898-1)
  2. Remote on/off via app — turn an entire circuit off from your phone
  3. Power monitoring — real-time current, voltage, energy, sometimes power factor

Some advanced WiFi MCBs add: earth-leakage protection (RCBO function), pre-paid energy mode, time-based scheduling, over/under voltage cut-off, and demand-response participation.

When you actually need a WiFi MCB

Use case 1: Geyser, air-conditioner, water motor on dedicated circuits

Each of these is a single load on a dedicated circuit. Remote on/off + monitoring at DB box level = ideal use case. Replace existing single-pole MCB with WiFi MCB. Now you can turn off the geyser from your phone when you're at work.

Use case 2: Rental property remote management

Landlord wants to disconnect a tenant's circuit during payment disputes (legal in some Pakistani provinces). WiFi MCB at DB box = remote enforcement without entering property.

Use case 3: Solar prosumer with selective load management

During grid outage, turn off non-essential circuits (AC, geyser) to extend battery runtime. WiFi MCBs at DB box give per-circuit selectivity.

Use case 4: Energy monitoring for tenant billing or BMS

Apartment / office buildings with shared utility — per-tenant WiFi MCB tracks actual consumption.

Use case 5: Pre-paid metering replacement

Some WiFi MCBs (CNC YCB10-W advanced model) support pre-paid balance — when credit runs out, MCB automatically opens.

When you don't need a WiFi MCB

  • Standard residential where you'll never remotely disconnect a circuit — regular MCB at PKR 450 does the job.
  • Circuits with multiple loads where individual control matters — use WiFi wall switches instead.
  • Solar inverter feed — NEPRA 2026 mandates Type B RCBO, not WiFi MCB. Use a regular Type B RCBO.

WiFi MCB voltage / under-voltage protection — what to verify

Pakistani grid voltage swings between 170V and 280V in many areas. A basic WiFi MCB without voltage protection will not trip on under/over voltage — your AC compressor still burns out. Always check:

  • Over-voltage cut-off: CNC YCB10-W trips at 265V. Tuya generic varies by firmware (some don't have it).
  • Under-voltage cut-off: CNC YCB10-W trips at 180V. Most Tuya generics don't support this.
  • Reset behaviour: Some WiFi MCBs require manual app reset after fault. CNC YCB10-W has auto-reset after voltage normalises (configurable delay).

If your area has wild voltage, pair the WiFi MCB with a dedicated voltage protector upstream.

WiFi MCB installation in Pakistani DB box

Step 1: Turn off main MCB at utility meter. Verify zero voltage with multimeter.

Step 2: Remove the existing circuit MCB you're replacing. Note which is live (phase) and neutral. Label them.

Step 3: Mount the WiFi MCB on the DIN rail (1 DIN slot for 1P, 2 slots for 2P or 1P+N).

Step 4: Connect:
— Top terminal: incoming phase (busbar from main)
— Bottom terminal: outgoing phase to load
— Some WiFi MCBs need neutral connection from busbar for the smart electronics. Verify the terminal layout from the manual.

Step 5: Turn on main MCB. WiFi MCB powers up; LED indicator goes blue (pairing mode).

Step 6: Install Tuya Smart Life or eWeLink app on your phone. Connect to home WiFi 2.4 GHz. Add device → scan QR code on MCB → pair.

Step 7: Test: turn off via app → verify load drops → turn on via app → verify load returns.

Step 8 (optional): Set up automations — timer for geyser, voltage cut-off rules, energy budget alerts.

Cost of installation: PKR 1,500-2,500 per WiFi MCB if your existing DB box has space. PKR 4,500-8,000 if DB box needs upgrade (deeper enclosure to fit thicker WiFi MCB modules).

Common WiFi MCB problems Pakistani buyers face

Problem 1: WiFi MCB disconnects from app after few hours. Cause: 2.4 GHz mesh router pushing the device to 5 GHz band. Fix: bind WiFi MCB to dedicated 2.4 GHz SSID, or disable band-steering on router.

Problem 2: WiFi MCB nuisance-trips during load shedding restoration. Cause: voltage swell at restoration exceeds the over-voltage cut-off threshold. Fix: install voltage protector upstream, or adjust cut-off threshold to 275V in app.

Problem 3: Power monitoring shows wrong reading. Cause: WiFi MCB current transformer mis-calibrated at factory. Fix: app has a calibration option; or contact distributor for replacement.

Problem 4: App turns off circuit but light stays on. Cause: switch loop wiring — load is wired to neutral instead of phase. Fix: rewire so WiFi MCB interrupts phase, not neutral.

Problem 5: WiFi MCB doesn't trip on actual short-circuit. Cause: counterfeit WiFi MCB with no real breaking mechanism (some Tuya generics are just contactors). Fix: only buy IEC 60898-1 certified — verify the certificate from manufacturer.

NEPRA 2026 + WiFi MCB

NEPRA 2026 Prosumer Regulations do not mandate WiFi MCB anywhere. But WiFi MCBs are useful for:

  • Net-metering customers who want to monitor self-consumption per circuit
  • Pre-paid tenant arrangements (Punjab + Sindh have pilot programs)
  • Load-control during peak hours (anticipating future Demand Response programs)

For the actual solar inverter feed, use a Type B RCBO (NEPRA mandatory) — not a WiFi MCB.

FAQ

Q: What is WiFi MCB?
A: A circuit breaker (MCB) with WiFi connectivity, app control, and power monitoring. Mounted on DIN rail in your DB box. Replaces a regular MCB.

Q: Is WiFi MCB safe?
A: Yes, when buying IEC 60898-1 certified models (CNC YCB10-W, Schneider Easy IoT, ABB iSCO). Counterfeit Tuya WiFi MCBs without proper breaking mechanism are not safe — verify certification.

Q: What is the difference between WiFi MCB and smart switch?
A: WiFi MCB sits in DB box and controls a whole circuit with overcurrent protection. Smart switch sits in wall and controls one load without protection.

Q: How much does WiFi MCB cost in Pakistan?
A: PKR 5,800-12,000 for 1P 16-32A from CNC, Tongou, Tuya generic. PKR 18,000-28,000 for Schneider or ABB premium models.

Q: Can WiFi MCB monitor electricity bill?
A: Yes — most WiFi MCBs track real-time kWh, daily/monthly totals, average current. Some calculate cost based on tariff input.

Q: Will WiFi MCB work without internet?
A: The MCB function (overcurrent protection) works without internet — it's a physical breaker. The smart features (app control, monitoring) need internet.

Q: Can I install WiFi MCB myself?
A: Only if you're a licensed electrician (PEC registered) or qualified DIY. DB-box work has serious shock risk — always isolate at main meter before working.

Q: Does CNC make a WiFi MCB?
A: Yes — CNC YCB10-W series at PKR 7,800-9,200 (1P 16-32A). IEC 60898-1 + 60947-2 compliant, 5-year warranty, works with Tuya / eWeLink / Home Assistant.
