What Are the 3 Types of Solar Inverters?

Off-Grid Solar Inverter

Works entirely with batteries — no WAPDA connection required. Your solar panels charge the battery bank; the inverter converts stored DC to AC for your home loads. Best for areas with 14–20+ hours of daily load-shedding or with no grid access at all (remote farms, villages, agricultural pump stations). The downside: you cannot earn NEPRA net metering credits, and battery replacement every 5–7 years adds long-term cost.

On-Grid Solar Inverter (Grid-Tied)

Connected to WAPDA only — no battery storage. Exports surplus solar power to the grid for NEPRA net metering credit (Rs 19.99/kWh buyback at peak in 2026). Safety feature: automatically shuts off during load-shedding (anti-islanding protection per IEC 62116). Best for DHA Karachi, Clifton, and F-7/F-8 Islamabad where load-shedding is under 4 hours daily. Fastest payback period of the three types.

Hybrid Solar Inverter

Combines grid connection with battery backup — the most popular choice in Pakistan in 2026. Charges batteries from solar or WAPDA, supplies your loads from solar first, then batteries, then grid. Qualifies for NEPRA net metering once batteries are full. More expensive than on-grid but delivers backup power during load-shedding. The correct choice for 85% of Pakistani households.

3-Step Inverter Sizing Calculator

Step 1 — List peak simultaneous loads:

Example home: 2 × 1.5-ton inverter ACs (2,400W total) + 4 ceiling fans (400W) + LED lights (200W) + fridge (200W) + misc appliances (300W) = 3,500W peak.

Step 2 — Apply 1.25× safety margin:

3,500W × 1.25 = 4,375W → round up to 5 kW inverter.

Step 3 — Battery sizing for hybrid/off-grid:

Formula: (Daily kWh needed during load-shedding) ÷ battery efficiency (0.85) ÷ DoD (0.8 for lithium) = battery Wh required. Example: 3,500W × 8 hours = 28 kWh ÷ 0.85 ÷ 0.8 = 41 kWh → four 48V 200Ah lithium battery units recommended.

NEPRA Net Metering: On-Grid vs Hybrid Comparison

Both on-grid and hybrid inverters qualify for NEPRA 2026 net metering. Key differences:

  • On-Grid: Exports 100% of surplus solar to grid. Best ROI if your monthly WAPDA bill exceeds Rs 20,000. Export rate 2026: Rs 19.99/kWh peak per NEPRA Determination 2024.
  • Hybrid: Fills batteries first; exports surplus. Export volume is lower, but you gain uninterrupted backup. The combined savings on bill + fuel generator costs typically beats on-grid ROI in Lahore and Multan.
  • ROI estimate: On-grid 3–5 years; hybrid 5–8 years (battery replacement at year 8–10 adds Rs 120,000–300,000).

Can I run my AC on a solar inverter in Pakistan?

Yes. A 1.5-ton inverter AC draws approximately 1,200–1,500W running load. For one AC, a 3 kW hybrid inverter is sufficient. For two ACs running simultaneously, choose a 5 kW or 8 kW hybrid inverter.

What is the difference between off-grid and hybrid solar inverter?

Off-grid inverters work with batteries only — no WAPDA connection. Hybrid inverters connect to both grid and batteries. If WAPDA is available even part of the day, hybrid is superior: it uses the grid to charge batteries when solar is insufficient, qualifies for NEPRA net metering, and provides automatic backup with no manual switching.
