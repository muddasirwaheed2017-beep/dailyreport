Which "curve" (B / C / D / K) to specify

Beyond the breaker type, IEC 60898-1 specifies tripping curve based on inrush characteristics of the protected load:

CurveMagnetic trip rangeBest for
B3-5× rated currentResistive loads — lighting, geyser, oven
C5-10× rated currentMixed loads — mainstream residential (most common in Pakistan)
D10-20× rated currentMotor starting, transformer inrush, capacitor banks
K8-12× rated currentSolar inverter inrush, UPS battery, welding plant

NEPRA 2026 rule changes affecting breaker selection

  • Type B RCBO is mandatory on AC + DC interfaces of all net-metered solar systems with battery storage (FY2026 effective)
  • Type 2 SPD required on both DC and AC sides per IEC 61643-31
  • DC MCB / MCCB rated to system DC voltage × 1.25 (1500V DC strings need 1500V-rated DC breaker)
  • Curve C minimum for grid-tied inverter output; curve K recommended for hybrid + battery systems
  • Annual trip test required on RCBO + RCCB for net-metered systems (record in maintenance log)
