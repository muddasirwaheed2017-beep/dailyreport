1. Why fridges fail without protection

Compressors are the most expensive component in any fridge — typically 50-70% of the total fridge cost. They fail in three ways:

  • Voltage sag — compressor draws higher current at 180V; windings overheat; insulation degrades
  • Voltage spike — start capacitor or running capacitor blown by 260V+ events
  • Frequent on-off cycling — when no protection means compressor restarts before its mandatory 3-minute cool-down, premature failure

4. Critical features for fridge protectors

Delay restart (the most important feature)

After power restoration, the protector should wait 3-5 minutes before reconnecting. Compressors need this time to equalise oil pressure; restarting too quickly damages the compressor. Most quality protectors have this; cheap ones don't.

Adjustable voltage thresholds

Default protectors trip at 170V (under) and 260V (over). Pakistani distribution often runs 180-250V routinely. Adjust to 175-255V to reduce nuisance trips while still catching real events.

Audible alarm (nice to have)

Beep when voltage is unsafe but before tripping. Lets you manually intervene if needed.

5. Installation

  1. Plug your fridge into the voltage protector.
  2. Plug the voltage protector into your standard wall socket.
  3. Verify the protector LED is green (normal operating state).
  4. For more elegant install: hardwire the protector into the dedicated fridge circuit in your DB-box (electrician work).
