**The most common Pakistan MCC wiring error that fails your SMETA buyer audit**

I've seen it in hundreds of Pakistan industrial panels: a Red pilot lamp glowing to indicate "motor is running."

It's wrong. And if you supply textile, food, or pharmaceutical factories targeting EU buyers, it's costing you audit non-conformances.

**IEC 60073 — the international colour code standard — is unambiguous:**

🔴 Red = Danger / Fault / Emergency ONLY
🟢 Green = Safe / Stopped / De-energised
🟠 Amber = Running / Auto / Abnormal (non-dangerous)
⬜ White = Power on / Neutral
🔵 Blue = Remote mode / Mandatory action

The Red-for-Running habit comes from 1970s British wiring practice that persisted in Pakistan after the standards were updated. SMETA, BSCI, ISO 45001, and OHSAS 18001 all treat it as a safety management system failure.

**Correct DOL starter pilot lamp layout:**
- White (top left): Control power present
- Green (top right): Motor STOPPED (safe to approach)
- Amber (bottom left): Motor RUNNING (caution)
- Red (bottom right): FAULT / Overload trip

This is the layout your EU buyer's HSE auditor expects to see.
