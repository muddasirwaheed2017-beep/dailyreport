**The single most common cause of tubewell motor failure in Pakistan is free to fix.**

Every year, Pakistani farmers lose hundreds of thousands of rupees to burned-out tubewell motors. The root cause in most cases isn't mechanical wear — it's single-phasing: one of the three utility phases drops during load-shedding switchover, and the motor runs on two phases, drawing 200–300% of rated current until the winding insulation fails.

The fix is a phase-failure relay — a component that costs Rs 1,200–3,500 and trips the motor contactor within 100 milliseconds of phase loss, before any winding damage occurs.

Yet the majority of tubewell control panels installed across Punjab, Sindh, and KPK lack even this basic protection.

**What a correctly specified tubewell control panel should include:**

✅ Phase-failure relay (trips on single-phasing AND phase reversal after LESCO reconnection)
✅ Thermal overload relay set to 105–110% of actual measured FLC
✅ IP65 or IP66 enclosure (not IP44 — Pakistan's monsoon will destroy an IP44 panel within two seasons)
✅ Star-delta starting for motors above 15 HP (reduces inrush to 33% of DOL, prevents DISCO feeder complaints)
✅ Dry-run protection (pressure switch or undercurrent relay — when the water table drops, the motor must not run dry)
✅ Ammeter with CT for real-time current monitoring
