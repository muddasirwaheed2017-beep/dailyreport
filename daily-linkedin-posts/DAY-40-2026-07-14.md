## Primary Post (Technical Authority Format)

**The conduit mistake that costs Pakistani factories Rs. 500,000+ in maintenance — and a Rs. 165 fix.**

I've seen the same failure pattern in Pakistani industrial facilities repeatedly:

Motor installed. Rigid GI conduit run straight to terminal box. Works fine for 12–18 months. Then the conduit cracks at the motor terminal box entry. Cable insulation exposed in a wet, dusty environment. Production stops. Emergency rewire.

The cause is almost never the cable or the motor. It's the conduit — specifically, the absence of 1.5 metres of flexible conduit at the motor terminal box.

Here's the physics: Your motor vibrates at 50Hz (or at VFD-commanded frequency). Your rigid conduit is fixed to the building structure. The motor terminal box moves microscopically relative to the fixed conduit, every cycle. That's 50 micro-movements per second. Over 12–18 months of 8-hour shifts, that's 700+ million stress cycles at the conduit-to-box joint. Metal fatigue is inevitable without a vibration-isolating flexible section.

The fix costs Rs. 165–270 per motor (1.5m of flexible conduit at Rs. 110–180/m). The production stoppage, emergency electrician, and rewire costs Rs. 50,000–500,000 depending on the motor and what it drives.

**The conduit decisions that matter most in Pakistan 2026:**

1. **Outdoor / rooftop runs:** Never use standard white PVC. South-facing walls reach 65–75°C in Pakistani summers — standard PVC is rated to 60°C. Use heavy-duty UV-resistant PVC (grey) or GI. Price difference: Rs. 55–85/m extra. Lifetime difference: 15 years vs 3–5 years.

2. **WAPDA commercial connections:** GI conduit is mandatory from metering point to main DB. PVC gets the application rejected — adding months to your connection timeline. GI 25mm costs Rs. 175–480/m depending on grade.

3. **Every motor terminal box:** 1.0–1.5m flexible conduit. Non-negotiable for any motor that runs continuously. Liquid-tight version for outdoor pumps and tubewell installations.

4. **Long outdoor runs:** Install expansion couplings every 10–12m. A 15m PVC conduit run expands 37mm between winter and summer in Pakistan. Without expansion couplings, junction boxes crack and saddles pull out.

5. **Conduit fill ratio:** The IEC standard is 40% maximum. In Pakistan's 45°C summer, the safe Pakistan practice is 35% maximum — cables in an overfilled conduit cannot dissipate heat at ambient temperatures we routinely see.
