## Version A — Technical Story (Long Form)

**The Emergency Stop Button That Failed: A Pakistan Panel Builder's Story**

A textile mill in Faisalabad wired an E-stop with a Normally Open (NO) contact.

The auditor from their EU buyer noticed it during a SMETA compliance audit. The finding: "Emergency stop not wired fail-safe. NC contact required per ISO 13849."

The production line was tagged out until the panel was rewired.

The cost: Rs. 45,000 panel rebuild + 3 days production loss.

The fix: one 1NC contact block instead of 1NO. Price difference: Rs. 150.

---

**This is the most expensive Pakistan panel wiring mistake:**

Emergency Stop must use a **Normally Closed (NC) contact** in series with the contactor coil.

Why? Because NC contact fail-safe means:
- Wiring breaks → contact opens → machine **STOPS** ✅
- Power fails → contact opens → machine **STOPS** ✅

With NO contact:
- Wiring breaks → contact stays open → machine **KEEPS RUNNING** ❌
- An E-stop button that can't stop a runaway machine is worse than no E-stop at all
