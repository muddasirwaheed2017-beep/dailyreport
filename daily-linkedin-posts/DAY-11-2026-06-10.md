**Are your NEPRA bills higher than they should be? The answer might be your power factor.**

Most Pakistani industrial units run at a power factor of 0.70–0.80. NEPRA requires you to maintain at least 0.85 — or face a reactive-power surcharge of 2–5 PKR per excess kVArh every billing cycle.

For a 200 kW factory running at 0.75 PF, that penalty quietly adds up to **PKR 300,000–400,000 per year.**

The fix: a correctly sized **power factor capacitor bank or APFC panel.**

---

**The sizing formula every engineer should know:**

**Qc (kVAR) = P (kW) × (tan φ₁ − tan φ₂)**

Example: 100 kW factory, PF 0.75 → target 0.95
→ Required: ~55 kVAR
→ Typical payback period: 8–14 months from avoided NEPRA surcharges alone

**Fixed vs. APFC — which do you need?**

✅ **Fixed bank:** Your load is stable, runs at consistent PF, single shift. Low upfront cost.

✅ **APFC panel:** Variable shifts, mixed loads, VFDs on site, or multiple motors starting at different times. Automatically switches capacitor steps to maintain target PF within ±0.02 at all load levels.

---

**Critical note for factories with VFDs, welding equipment, or UPS:**

Plain capacitors in harmonic-rich environments fail in 2–3 years. Add a **7% detuned reactor** (tuned at 189 Hz) to block 5th harmonic amplification. It adds 25–35% to unit cost — and multiplies capacitor life by 4–5×.
