## Post 1 — Primary (Professional / Panel builder audience)

**The component Pakistan panel builders underspend on — until the PLC resets at 2am.**

The 24VDC DIN rail power supply (SMPS PSU) is the supply source for everything in your control circuit:
→ Pilot lamps (0.05A each)
→ Interposing relay coils (0.06–0.08A each)
→ PLC CPU and I/O cards (0.3–1.5A per card)
→ Proximity and inductive sensors (0.02–0.05A each)
→ Pushbutton LEDs, timer relays, solenoid valve coils

When the PSU fails — or worse, starts drooping output at 22.8V instead of 24V — everything connected to it behaves strangely. Intermittent PLC DI false inputs. Sensors chattering. Relays not picking up cleanly. Weeks of fault-finding before someone checks the PSU output voltage.

**The Pakistan-specific issues that kill cheap DIN rail PSUs:**

1️⃣ WAPDA reconnection spikes (260–290V for 50–200ms) — narrow-input PSUs (180–264VAC) trip repeatedly until their filter capacitors degrade. Wide-range 85–265VAC input absorbs these spikes by design.

2️⃣ Pakistan summer heat derating — panel interior reaches 60–75°C in July. Cheap PSUs use 85°C-rated electrolytic capacitors rated for ~3,000 hours at panel temperature. Quality PSUs use 105°C caps: 30,000+ hours at the same temperature.

3️⃣ No fold-back OCP — a shorted sensor cable takes down the entire 24VDC rail if there are no sub-circuit MCBs and the PSU has fuse-only protection (not auto-recovery OCP).

**The 70% Pakistan derating rule (most ignored rule in panel building):**
A 10A DIN rail PSU in a Pakistan panel delivers 7A safely in summer. Size to 70% max loading. Aim for 60% if the panel has no forced ventilation.

## Post 2 — Short version (Personal profile)

Most common mistake in Pakistan control panel wiring I see repeatedly:

A 10A 24VDC DIN rail PSU loaded to 9A, inside a panel that reaches 70°C in July.

That's a 90% load at +30°C above the IEC standard test temperature. The electrolytic capacitors are running at reduced life.

Not immediately — gradually. Six to eighteen months later, the panel starts behaving strangely. PLC inputs triggering without cause. Pilot lamps flickering. Eventually the PSU fails completely.

The fix is simple: derate to 70% in Pakistan summer. A 10A PSU → maximum 7A continuous load in a panel without forced air cooling.

A 10A wide-range Meanwell-class PSU in Pakistan: Rs. 6,500–9,500.

The cost of an unplanned shutdown to replace a failed PSU plus troubleshoot the phantom faults it caused: Rs. 15,000–100,000 depending on the process.

Size correctly. The 70% rule is not conservative — it's the minimum for reliable Pakistan operation.
