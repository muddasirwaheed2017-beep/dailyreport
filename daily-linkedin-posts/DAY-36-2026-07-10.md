## Long-form post (primary)

**The most under-specified component in Pakistani electrical panels — and why it causes the most failures.**

A Rs 25 plastic cable gland.

Every electrical panel has dozens of them. Almost none are specified correctly.

Here's what I see on site in Pakistan, repeatedly:

**The three mistakes that cause 80% of panel failures:**

1. **Wrong size** — Electrical engineer specifies "PG13.5 for 4mm² cable." Electrician orders PG13.5. The 4mm² SWA cable arrives on site with an OD of 20mm. PG13.5 clamping range is 6–12mm. The gland won't fit. Site delay. Emergency order. Project penalty clause triggered.

   *Root cause:* Cable gland size must be based on cable OUTER DIAMETER, not conductor cross-section. A 4mm² cable can be anywhere from 9mm to 22mm OD depending on whether it's plain, armoured, multicore, or twin-and-earth.

2. **Wrong IP rating** — Standard plastic gland installed in outdoor distribution box. July monsoon arrives. Water wicks down the cable jacket through the cracked rubber seal (cracked in summer heat, not noticed). Ground fault. MCB trips. Factory shutdown at 11pm.

   *Fix:* Outdoor DB box = brass IP66 minimum. Flood-risk / ground-level = IP68. Not optional in Pakistan.

3. **Armour not earthed at second end** — Armoured cable from panel to motor, 75m run. Armour earthed at panel (source end). Not earthed at motor terminal box. 3 months in: VFD starts nuisance-tripping. Fault code "output phase imbalance." Engineer spends a day chasing the VFD. Root cause: armour on 75m run has induced 12V AC on it from the motor's output frequency. This induced voltage is coupling back into the VFD's output filter.

   *Fix:* Earth the armour at BOTH ends using a Type E1W armoured cable gland. Verify continuity armour-to-earth bar &lt;0.1Ω during commissioning.

**What this costs:**
- Plastic PG11 (most common): Rs 25–55
- Brass PG16 (outdoor standard): Rs 200–420
- Armoured M25 SWA gland: Rs 680–1,400
- IP68 M20 for pump/tubewell: Rs 380–750
- IECEx for hazardous area (Karachi refinery, CNG): Rs 3,500–15,000+

The difference between correct and incorrect gland specification on a typical 100-circuit industrial panel: Rs 40,000 in upgraded glands. Cost of one panel flood in a pharmaceutical clean room or textile factory: Rs 800,000–3,000,000 in lost production, rewiring, WAPDA non-compliance rectification.
