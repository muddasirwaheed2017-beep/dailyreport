**Cable Tray in Pakistan 2026: The Complete Procurement Guide for Electrical Engineers**

If you're spec-ing a cable tray system for a factory, solar plant, or commercial building in Pakistan, here's what you need to know before you raise a PO.

**The four types in plain English:**

🔶 **Perforated GI tray** — The workhorse. Holes for heat dissipation, solid sides for protection. Covers 80% of industrial applications. Rs 350–1,800/metre depending on width.

🔶 **Ladder tray** — Maximum ventilation for large-diameter power cables (70mm² and above). Mandatory for heavy industrial and solar power plants. Rs 850–4,500/metre.

🔶 **Wire mesh basket** — Data centres and server rooms. Easy to cut on-site, excellent for Cat6/fibre. Rs 600–1,500/metre.

🔶 **Solid bottom tray** — Instrument and signal cables that need EMI shielding and drip protection. Used in chemical and food processing plants.

**The one rule most contractors get wrong: the 40% fill rule.**

Per NEC 392 and NEPRA guidelines, cable cross-sections should occupy no more than 40% of the usable tray area. I've seen installations at 80%+ fill — this creates dangerous heat accumulation and is a fire risk.

Quick guide:
- Up to 10 cables (≤16mm²) → 100mm tray
- 10–20 cables → 200mm tray
- 20–40 cables or large feeders → 300–600mm ladder tray

**Material selection for Pakistan's climate:**

Pre-galvanized (electro-zinc) trays rust within 2–3 years in Karachi's coastal humidity. Specify **Hot-Dip Galvanized (HDG)** — 85–100 µm zinc coating, 15–25 year service life.
