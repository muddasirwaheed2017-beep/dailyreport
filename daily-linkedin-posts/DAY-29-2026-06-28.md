**Three things I see wired wrong in Pakistani electrical installations every week:**

**1. Motor wired directly through the timer contact**
Timer contacts are rated 16A at 240V. A 2HP motor draws 14–16A at start — right at the rating limit, for repeated starts daily. Use the timer to switch a contactor coil (20–50 VA). The contactors handles the motor current.

**2. Star-delta timer set to 3–4 seconds**
The motor hasn't reached 80% speed before delta transition. The current surge trips the overload relay and — over dozens of repeated starts — burns the winding end-turns. Safe default: 8 seconds for motors up to 15kW.

**3. Astro timer DST setting left ON**
Pakistan does not observe Daylight Saving Time. European-market astro timers auto-shift +1 hour in summer. Result: mosque gate lights come on 1 hour late from March to October. Disable DST in the timer setup menu.
