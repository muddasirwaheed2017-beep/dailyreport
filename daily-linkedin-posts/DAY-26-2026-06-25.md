## Motor Contactor Selection in Pakistan: The Complete 2026 Guide for Electrical Engineers

Every motor installation in Pakistan — from a 1 HP tubewell pump in rural Punjab to a 30 HP compressor in a Karachi textile mill — runs through the same three-component protection stack: a circuit breaker, a magnetic contactor, and a thermal overload relay. Getting even one of these wrong costs you a motor rewind, a production shutdown, or both.

Here is what you need to know in 2026.

**The AC3 Rating Problem**

The single most costly mistake we see in Pakistan motor panels is using a contactor's AC1 current rating to size for motor loads. A contactor labelled "25A" has a 25A AC1 rating (resistive loads, cos φ > 0.95). Its AC3 rating — the figure that actually matters for squirrel-cage induction motors — is typically 9–12A on the same contactor. If you connect a 22A FLC motor to that "25A" contactor under AC3 conditions, the contacts will weld shut within weeks.

Always specify the AC3 rated operational current. Confirm it from the manufacturer's datasheet, not the label.

**Pakistan Contactor Prices 2026**

AC contactor prices range from PKR 800–1,600 for a 9A (up to 5.5 HP / 4 kW), PKR 1,100–2,200 for an 18A (up to 10 HP / 7.5 kW), and PKR 4,000–7,500 for a 65A (up to 40 HP / 30 kW). IEC 60947-4-1 certified contactors cost 20–35% more than uncertified imports but carry a rated service life of 1 million AC3 operations — versus 100,000–300,000 for uncertified alternatives.

Motor overload relays range from PKR 500–900 for small ranges (0.6–2.5A) to PKR 1,800–3,500 for 30–40A ranges suitable for 18.5 kW / 25 HP motors.

**DOL vs Star-Delta: The Pakistan Decision**

Direct Online (DOL) starters are appropriate for motors up to 7.5 kW (10 HP) on strong urban WAPDA feeders. Starting current is 5–7× Full Load Current, but on a properly sized branch circuit this is manageable and the simplicity justifies it: 1 contactor + 1 overload relay + MCB.

For motors above 10 HP — and especially for rural agricultural tubewell connections where the WAPDA feeder is already marginal — a Star-Delta starter reduces starting current to 33% of the DOL value. This requires 3 contactors, a timer relay, and proper electrical interlock wiring. Component cost is approximately 3× the DOL equivalent, but the reduction in feeder voltage dip and motor thermal stress pays back quickly in motor life extension.

**The WAPDA Phase Failure Problem**

Pakistan's 3-phase overhead distribution network is vulnerable to single-phase faults — a blown fuse, a line break, a loose feeder connection. When one phase is lost, a running induction motor continues to rotate on the remaining two phases, drawing 170–200% of full-load current. A thermal overload relay with differential sensitivity will trip within 20–60 minutes. By then, insulation degradation has already occurred.

For all tubewell installations — urban or rural — add a dedicated phase-failure and phase-sequence relay alongside your contactor. Cost is PKR 800–2,500. Motor rewind cost in Pakistan is PKR 15,000–80,000 for common sizes. The economics are straightforward.

**The Complete Motor Protection Stack**

A correctly protected 3-phase motor in Pakistan requires four layers:

1. **Short-circuit protection**: 3-pole MCB (C-curve) or MCCB at 200–250% of FLC
2. **Switching and fail-safe**: AC magnetic contactor — drops out on loss of control power or stop command
3. **Overload and single-phasing protection**: Thermal OLR or electronic motor protection relay, set at 100–115% of nameplate FLC
4. **Phase protection**: Phase-failure/sequence relay for rural and critical applications; SPD for monsoon outdoor panels

None of these components replaces the others. Each addresses a failure mode the others cannot handle.
