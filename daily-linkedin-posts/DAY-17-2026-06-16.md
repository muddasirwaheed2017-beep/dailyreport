We audited 40+ LED lighting failures in Pakistani commercial installations over the last two years.

The most common cause of early failure was not LED chip quality or heat management.

It was driver specification error.

Specifically: constant-voltage drivers used for constant-current loads.

Here is how it happens:

The electrician sees "12V, 30W" on an LED driver used for a strip project. A new panel light arrives. The panel light datasheet says "30W" on the box. The electrician connects it to the existing 12V driver.

The panel light has no internal current limiting. With 12V applied directly, it draws uncontrolled current. It runs hot. Within 3–6 months it fails — taking the fitting with it.

The correct specification for that panel light was a 350 mA or 700 mA constant-current driver with an output voltage range covering the fixture's forward voltage (typically 28–42V).

---

**Three specification rules that eliminate 80% of LED driver failures in Pakistan:**

**1. Match driver type to fixture type, not wattage.**
Check the fixture label. "350 mA" or "700 mA" = constant current driver. "12 VDC input" or "24 VDC" = constant voltage driver.

**2. Derate for Pakistani ambient temperature.**
Driver manufacturers rate at 25°C. Our summer ceilings reach 50–60°C. Use maximum 80% of rated wattage in ventilated ceiling voids. Use 70% maximum in sealed false ceilings. A 75W driver for a 54W load is correct specification, not over-engineering.

**3. Select IP67 minimum for any outdoor application.**
IP65 is defeated by monsoon wind-driven rain on exposed walls. IP67 drivers (rated for 1m/30min immersion) are the right choice for any external Pakistani installation.

Pakistan's LED lighting market is growing rapidly as load-shedding forces businesses into self-generation with solar + battery setups. LED efficiency matters more when every watt comes from your own solar array.

Getting the driver specification right is not a technical luxury. It is the difference between a 7-year installation and a 7-month replacement cycle.
