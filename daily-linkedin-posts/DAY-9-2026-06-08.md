**Every exposed wire splice is a fire waiting to happen. Here's what the NEPRA 2026 inspection checklist says about junction boxes — and what most Pakistani electricians still get wrong.**

A junction box costs between Rs 80 and Rs 250. A house fire caused by an unprotected wire splice costs everything.

Yet on NEPRA's 2026 occupancy inspection checklist, "exposed or un-enclosed wire splices" remains one of the most commonly flagged Critical Faults — particularly in residential construction in Karachi, Lahore and Rawalpindi.

Here is what the standard requires:

✅ All wire splices in concealed wiring must be inside an IEC 60670-certified junction box
✅ Bathroom and damp-area boxes must be rated minimum IP44
✅ Outdoor and solar installations require minimum IP65 polycarbonate enclosures
✅ Solar DC combiner boxes above 3 kW arrays must include DC SPD (surge protection) per IEC 62109-1

**The three mistakes we see most often:**

1. PVC boxes used outdoors → UV degradation in 2–3 years → water ingress → fault
2. Open knockouts left unfilled → IP rating is void the moment the installation is complete
3. Wrong box size → conductors crammed in → insulation damage → arc fault
