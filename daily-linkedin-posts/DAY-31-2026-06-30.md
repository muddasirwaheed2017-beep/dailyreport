**Three calibration decisions that prevent 90% of photocell failures in Pakistan:**

**1. Lux threshold for your city:**
— Lahore/Karachi (urban light pollution): Turn ON at 20–25 lux, Turn OFF at 70–80 lux
— Faisalabad/Multan (dusty): Turn ON at 25–30 lux — dust on the lens reduces sensitivity progressively
— Monsoon season (July–September): Add 30-second minimum time delay to prevent cloud-cover false triggering

**2. Mounting direction:**
Face the photocell sensor north. Away from the lamp it controls. Away from direct sun. A photocell facing its own lamp will cycle on/off every few seconds — this is not a fault, it is an installation error.

**3. Load rating:**
LED street lights have inrush current 10–20x higher than their steady-state rating. A photocell rated at the lamp's nameplate current will fail within weeks. Always rate the photocell at 2x the lamp's steady-state current, or use an industrial controller with a separate contactor.

---

**Photocell vs Astro Timer — the question we get most from Pakistani facility managers:**

Use a photocell when: you want lights to respond to actual ambient light levels (gate lights, path lights, simple outdoor areas).

Use an astro timer when: you need precise time control, want to turn lights off at midnight to save electricity, or have loads that must not respond to weather (tubewell motor schedules, signage).

Use both when: you want dawn-to-dusk photocell control PLUS a midnight cut-off for half the lighting circuit. Standard configuration in large Pakistani industrial estates.

---

**The wiring mistake that creates a live lamp when the light appears to be "off":**

Some Pakistani electricians wire the photocell in the neutral conductor to avoid working on the live side. The photocell switches correctly — the lamp goes dark — but the lamp fitting remains live at 220V.

The result: a maintenance worker touches the lamp assuming it is safe because it is off. It is not.

Always switch the live (black/brown) conductor. Never switch neutral. This is not optional — it is a safety requirement under any valid electrical standard.
