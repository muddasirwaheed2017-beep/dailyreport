## Topic: Phase Sequence Relay / Phase Monitoring Relay Pakistan

---

### LinkedIn Post (Technical — targeting electrical engineers, contractors, facility managers)

**"The Rs 3,000 device that Pakistan's electrical industry consistently forgets to specify"**

I've reviewed motor failure reports from across Pakistan's industrial and agricultural sectors. The pattern is consistent:

A 3-phase induction motor burns.
Post-mortem: "Phase loss."
The thermal overload relay did not trip in time.

Why? Because a thermal overload relay is designed for mechanical overload. Its trip time for phase loss is 2–10 minutes via the current asymmetry model. In that window, a DOL-started motor running single-phase draws 173% FLA, reaches Class F thermal limit in under 3 minutes, and destroys its winding insulation.

A phase monitoring relay detects the same phase loss in 0.1–3 seconds and opens the contactor. The motor survives.

**The Pakistan-specific problem:**

WAPDA's load shedding schedule creates a systematic hazard that doesn't exist in most other countries: frequent phase loss events followed by reconnection, often with wrong phase sequence after maintenance. During summer, rural 11kV feeders in Punjab and Sindh regularly deliver 320–350V (80–87% of nominal 400V), causing chronic low-voltage stress.

Every 3-phase motor installation on WAPDA supply should include:
1. ✅ AC magnetic contactor (switching)
2. ✅ Thermal/electronic overload relay (current protection)
3. ✅ Phase monitoring relay (voltage protection — both are needed, they cover different failure modes)
4. ✅ Float switch / dry-run protection (for pump installations)

The overload relay and phase monitoring relay are not alternatives. They are complementary — and both are required for a correctly protected motor starter.

**Under-voltage trip setting for WAPDA:**

A common misconfiguration: setting the trip threshold at 90% (360V) of nominal. On any rural feeder during summer peak hours, this causes nuisance trips every evening.

Practical settings:
- Urban, stable feeder: 350V trip, 3–5s delay
- Peri-urban: 340V trip, 5–10s delay
- Rural Punjab/Sindh agricultural: 320–330V trip, 10–15s delay

The goal is to ride through WAPDA reconnection transients (5–10 seconds of unstable voltage) while still protecting against sustained low-voltage operation.
