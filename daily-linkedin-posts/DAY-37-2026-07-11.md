## Primary Long-Form Post

**The most common wiring mistake in Pakistani motor control panels — and it bypasses your overload protection entirely.**

I've reviewed hundreds of pump panel wiring diagrams from Lahore, Karachi, and Faisalabad. One mistake appears in roughly 40% of panels built by non-specialised electricians:

**The overload relay NC contact is wired after the cam switch LOCAL output.**

Here's why this matters:

In most tubewell and industrial motor panels, the cam switch has three positions: LOCAL (manual pushbutton control), OFF, and REMOTE (float switch or PLC control).

When the overload relay is wired correctly, its NC contact is in the **common supply line** — before the cam switch. This means an overload trip cuts the contactor coil circuit regardless of which cam switch position is selected.

When it's wired incorrectly (after the LOCAL output), the protection only works in LOCAL mode. Switch to REMOTE mode — the most common operating mode for tubewell pumps running on float switches at night — and the overload relay is effectively bypassed. The motor can run to destruction with no trip, no alarm.

I've seen submersible pump motors burn out in summer with this fault. The pump ran overnight on REMOTE, the float switch failed closed, water kept pumping, the motor overheated — and nothing tripped.

**The fix:** A single re-routing of the overload NC contact from the LOCAL output wire back to the common supply rail. Ten minutes of rework that makes your protection unconditional.

**Rating selection rule for Pakistan:**
Always go one standard size above your calculated FLC. WAPDA feeder voltage dips to 340V — what's 15A at 400V becomes 18–19A at 340V. A 25A cam switch on a 10 HP motor will fail in under 18 months from heat cycling.

**The AC-23A rule:**
Generic Chinese cam switches often lack an IEC utilisation category marking. If it's not marked AC-23A (motor switching duty), don't use it to switch motor loads. AC-21A switches weld contacts on motor inrush current. AC-23A breaks up to 8× FLC safely.
