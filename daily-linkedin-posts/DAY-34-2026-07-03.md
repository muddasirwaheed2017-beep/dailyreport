---

**Here's what WAPDA actually requires for LT industrial revenue metering in Pakistan:**

✅ Accuracy class 0.5S (not 1.0 — the "S" is mandatory)
✅ Burden matched to cable run (2.5mm² copper, keep secondary under 15m for a 5VA CT)
✅ CT ratio matching the energy meter's programmed ratio exactly
✅ CT shorting terminal block before the meter (so you can replace meters without open-circuiting the CT)

Miss any of these and you're either:
→ Paying for electricity you didn't use, or
→ Getting caught by a WAPDA meter inspector

**The CT mistake I see most in Pakistan panels:**

Using a protection CT (5P20) for revenue metering because "it was available."

Protection CTs are designed to stay linear at fault currents (up to 20× rated). At normal operating currents (50%–100% of rating), their composite error can be 3%+ — this directly translates to billing error.

Conversely, using a metering CT (0.5S) on an overcurrent relay: the metering CT saturates by design at 120% rated current to protect the meter. Your relay sees zero secondary current during a fault. The motor burns.

One CT type. Two completely different jobs. Never substitute.

## Short-form Version (for mobile / feed scroll)

**The CT rule every Pakistan panel engineer needs to know:**

WAPDA revenue metering = class 0.5S CT (not 1.0, not protection class 5P)

Protection relay panel = class 5P10 or 5P20 CT (not metering class)

They are not interchangeable. Using the wrong one either gives you bad billing or bad protection — sometimes both.

CT prices in Pakistan 2026: PKR 350 to PKR 6,500 depending on ratio, class, and type.
