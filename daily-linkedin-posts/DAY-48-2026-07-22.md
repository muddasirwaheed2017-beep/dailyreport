**The motor burnt out. The thermal overload relay never tripped. Here's why.**

A Faisalabad textile mill owner contacted us in August last year. His 22 kW ring-frame motor had failed — windings shorted, rotor surface discoloured from sustained high temperature. The thermal overload relay was intact, properly calibrated, and had never tripped.

We measured his WAPDA supply at 14:30 on a Thursday in July.

R phase: 378 V
Y phase: 362 V
B phase: 388 V

Voltage asymmetry: 4.2%.

The motor had been running with one winding absorbing roughly 30% more current than the other two — every afternoon from June through September — for three years. The thermal overload relay, which averages current across all three bimetal strips, saw a combined current well within its trip threshold. It never triggered.

The winding insulation failed quietly, over thousands of hours, one Pakistani summer at a time.

---

**What voltage asymmetry actually does (and why most panel builders don't know)**

NEMA's rule of thumb: 1% voltage unbalance → 6–10% current unbalance.

At 4% voltage asymmetry (the mill's situation):
→ ~30% current unbalance in the hottest winding
→ That winding runs ~20°C above the others
→ Winding insulation class H (180°C rated) degrades twice as fast at 200°C vs 180°C
→ Motor life reduced by 50–60% just from this single, persistent, invisible condition

This isn't a rare event. WAPDA summer industrial feeder data from FESCO and LESCO published zones shows 3–8% asymmetry as a routine afternoon condition in June–September across Lahore, Faisalabad, Sialkot, and Rawalpindi.

**What to set (Pakistan WAPDA, DOL starters)**

Under-voltage trip: **330 V** (87% of 380 V nominal)
Over-voltage trip: **430 V** (113% — catches load-shedding return surge)
Asymmetry trip: **3%** for motors ≥11 kW / **5%** for ≤7.5 kW
Trip time delay: **3–5 seconds** (filters WAPDA switching transients)
Return delay: **5–10 seconds** (waits for supply stabilisation)

The time delay is critical. Without it, every WAPDA load-shedding event causes a nuisance trip — and operators disable the relay within a week. The 3-second delay filters the 1–2 second switching transients while still catching genuine sustained faults.

---

**One wiring rule: supply side only**

The relay sensing terminals (L1, L2, L3) must connect to the supply side of the main contactor — before the contactor, not after it.

Motor-side connection gives the relay back-EMF readings during motor coast-down. It sees false voltages, misses real faults, and the installer assumes everything is working. Correct wiring takes 5 minutes. Wrong wiring makes the relay useless.

---

**Auto-restart vs manual reset — a safety classification decision**

Auto-restart: ✅ appropriate for pumps, cooling tower fans, HVAC — unmanned equipment where loss of service for 30+ seconds causes process damage or product loss. Standard practice for Pakistan chiller water pumps and irrigation systems.

Manual reset: ✅ mandatory for lathes, mills, grinders, compressors, any machine with rotating tooling. PSQCA and ILO guidelines both require it. An unexpected restart after a load-shedding event has caused serious injuries in Pakistan workshops. Wire it correctly from the start.
