## Draft A (Educational / Installer-Focused)

**The cable termination mistake that's quietly killing Pakistani solar installations**

I've seen it in the field across dozens of rooftop solar projects in Lahore, Karachi, and Islamabad:

Installers use standard compression lugs rated for 70°C on DC cables running at 60°C ambient — which means the cable insulation is operating at 5–10°C below its thermal limit, 8–10 hours per day, every day.

Three to five years in, insulation degrades. Resistance climbs. Hot spots develop. In the worst cases, arcing under the module array where nobody can see it.

The fix is straightforward — it's just not well-known:

✅ Use IEC 61238-1 certified compression lugs (not press-fit or unrated)
✅ Match lug material to cable material (bi-metal Cu-Al for aluminium feeders)
✅ Apply DIN 46235 crimping die sizes — not "close enough" hex dies
✅ Torque the bolt to specification (M8 terminal: 12–15 N·m)
✅ Use heat-shrink insulation on the barrel after crimping
