**A Rs. 350 component protects a Rs. 150,000 brain. Most Pakistan panel shops skip it.**

In every Pakistan MCC panel, PLC cabinet, and fire alarm control system, there is a hierarchy:

The intelligent layer: the PLC CPU, the fire panel controller, the SCADA terminal.
The power layer: the contactors, solenoid valves, motor windings, 220V/440V circuits.

These two worlds must never touch the same conductor.

The interposing relay is the wall between them.

---

**The Pakistan problem no one talks about:**

Pakistan experiences 3–12 power interruptions per day. Each grid-to-generator transfer generates a voltage transient of 600–1,200 volts lasting microseconds.

That transient travels through your control circuit. Into your contactor coil wiring. Into your PLC output terminal.

If you have a Rs. 350 interposing relay on that output: the relay contact takes the hit. The PLC sees nothing.

If you don't: the PLC output transistor absorbs 600V. The transistor fails. Sometimes immediately. Sometimes after 50 cycles — a slow, intermittent fault that takes three service visits to diagnose.

PLC output card replacement: Rs. 80,000–400,000.
The relay that would have prevented it: Rs. 350.

---

**Pakistan panel builders: the one step that is almost always skipped**

The freewheeling diode across the relay coil.

When a 24VDC relay coil is de-energised, its magnetic field collapses and generates an inductive kickback voltage of 100–200V on the coil terminals — in reverse.

That spike goes straight back down the wire to the PLC output transistor.

A Rs. 5 diode (1N4007) across the A1-A2 relay coil terminals clamps this spike to 0.7V.

Without it: the PLC output transistor degrades with every switching cycle. 10,000 cycles in a busy Pakistan factory = months of service before the failure appears.

This is the single most common, entirely preventable PLC output failure in Pakistan industrial plants.
