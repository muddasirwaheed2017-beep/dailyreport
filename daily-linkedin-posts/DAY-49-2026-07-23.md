## Long-Form Post

**The 300 mA earth leakage relay that killed a Pakistan worker**

A Gujranwala garment factory had a standard problem: their 30 mA RCCB at the main sub-distribution board kept tripping every time WAPDA power came back after load-shedding.

The electrical contractor diagnosed it as "nuisance trip." The fix: replace the 30 mA RCCB with a 300 mA unit.

Six months later, during a routine production run, a machine operator received an electric shock from a metal enclosure. 240 V, faulty motor cable insulation, earth fault current well below 300 mA.

The 300 mA device never tripped. It wasn't designed to — 300 mA is equipment protection only, not personal protection. The IEC 60364 ventricular fibrillation threshold is 30 mA. The contractor had installed a device ten times too insensitive for human protection.

This is the most dangerous mistake in Pakistan electrical panel work. And it's common.

---

**Why sensitivity selection matters in Pakistan**

Earth leakage relays and RCCBs are not interchangeable by sensitivity. Each value has a defined protective role:

**30 mA** → Personal (shock) protection. Required at all Pakistan residential socket circuits, wet areas, construction sites. IEC 60364-4-41 and Pakistan Electrical Safety Code both require ≤30 mA at socket outlets. This is not negotiable.

**100 mA** → Fire protection. At 100–300 mA, a fault arc can sustain long enough to ignite PVC cable insulation in conduit or ceiling voids. A 100 mA RCCB or ELR on sub-distribution feeders prevents incendiary arcs without nuisance-tripping every circuit.

**300 mA** → Equipment protection only. Protects cables, motors, and transformers from sustained insulation faults. Does NOT protect persons.

The correct response to a nuisance-tripping 30 mA RCCB is not to increase sensitivity. It is to find and fix the fault, or — if the trips are WAPDA load-shedding inrush — to install an ELR with adjustable time delay (100–200 ms) at the feeder or incomer level while keeping 30 mA instantaneous at the final circuits.

---

**The Pakistan earthing system question nobody asks**

Before specifying any earth leakage device, you must know which earthing system feeds your site. In Pakistan, there are three:

**TN-C-S** — Most urban commercial sites in Lahore (LESCO), Faisalabad (FESCO), Gujranwala (GEPCO). The DISCO provides a combined neutral-earth (PEN) conductor. Earth fault current in this system is 1,000–5,000 A — enough to trip MCBs and MCCBs. RCCBs here provide personal protection against sub-threshold faults, as backup protection, and in wet areas.

**TT** — The dominant system in rural and peri-urban MEPCO, PESCO, HESCO, QESCO areas. The consumer provides their own earth electrode, independent of the DISCO neutral. Earth fault current = 240 V / (consumer R_E + source impedance). With a typical Pakistan clay-soil electrode at 10–20 Ω, fault current is 8–16 A. No MCB will reliably trip on 8–16 A through an 80 A or 100 A feeder MCB.

In a TT system, an RCCB or ELR is the ONLY earth fault protection. This includes the majority of Pakistan tube-well pump panels, agricultural transformer sites, and rural industrial installations.

**IT** — Hospital OTs, critical care rooms, UPS systems. Requires insulation monitoring device (IMD), not a conventional RCCB.

If you're working on a MEPCO, PESCO, HESCO, or QESCO site and you haven't measured your earth electrode resistance — you don't know what protection your site has.

---

**RCCB vs Earth Leakage Relay — when to use which**

This is another Pakistan panel builder confusion point.

**RCCB** (Residual Current Circuit Breaker): Self-contained device with integrated sensing and breaking contacts. Maximum 125 A. Built-in breaking capacity 6–10 kA. Cannot add time delay (its fast trip speed is the protective function for 30 mA personal protection). PKR 800–6,500 depending on rating and sensitivity.

**ELR** (Earth Leakage Relay): Relay only. Senses via separate toroid CT. Trips any external contactor, MCCB, or breaker. Unlimited current rating. Adjustable sensitivity and time delay. Used for all feeders above 125 A, industrial MCCs, bus protection, and any installation where WAPDA restoration inrush is a nuisance trip source. PKR 2,500–18,000 (relay only) plus PKR 1,000–4,500 (toroid CT).

For WAPDA load-shedding nuisance trips: set 100–200 ms time delay on ELR at feeder and incomer level. The 100 ms delay filters WAPDA restoration transients (capacitor inrush, back-EMF) while preserving 30 mA instantaneous protection at final circuits.

---

**The toroid CT wiring rule most Pakistan electricians get wrong**

Pass L1, L2, L3, and Neutral through the toroid CT. That's four conductors.

Do NOT pass the Earth (PE) conductor through the toroid.

If the PE conductor passes through the toroid, the fault current on it creates a magnetic flux that opposes the residual flux from the live conductors. The relay never trips — even on a solid earth fault. This is the most common ELR wiring error in Pakistan panels and it creates a completely non-functional installation.

Also: earth armoured cable at one end only (at the distribution board). Double-earthed armouring creates circulating current through the toroid and causes nuisance trips.
