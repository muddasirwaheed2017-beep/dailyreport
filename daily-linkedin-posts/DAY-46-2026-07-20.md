**Pakistan panel cooling is not a luxury. It's the most cost-effective maintenance you can do.**

What most engineers get wrong:

**1. Wrong thermostat setpoint**
European manuals say: set ON at 40°C.
Pakistan rule: set ON at 35°C.
At 40°C ON setting, your panel interior is already at 65°C before the fan starts. VFD IGBTs are 10–15 minutes from a thermal trip.

**2. Ignoring filter mat replacement**
In a Faisalabad textile mill, filter mats need replacing every 2–4 weeks.
In a cement plant: every 1–2 weeks.
Most engineers replace them when the fan fails — by then, the panel has been running at 20% airflow for weeks.

**3. IP42 on outdoor panels**
One monsoon season destroys the fan motor windings.
IP65 is mandatory for any outdoor panel in Pakistan.

**4. Inlet and outlet fans on the same panel wall**
Airflow short-circuits. The rest of the panel stays hot.
Inlet at bottom → outlet at top. Always.

---

**Fan sizing formula (Pakistan context):**

Q (m³/h) = 3.1 × P_heat (W) / ΔT

For a 22kW VFD panel:
→ P_heat = 440W (at 2% VFD loss)
→ ΔT = 10°C target (panel interior = ambient + 10°C)
→ Q = 3.1 × 440 / 10 = 136 m³/h minimum
→ Add 30% for filter pressure drop: 177 m³/h
→ Select: 230mm fan rated 280+ m³/h ✓
