2. 4-question Pakistani buyer framework

Skip the marketing copy. Answer these four questions in order and the architecture picks itself.

Q1 — Do you have grid power on your site?

  • No: off-grid is the only valid choice. Go straight to section 4.
  • Yes: continue to Q2.

Q2 — Does your area experience load-shedding (more than 30 minutes per day average)?

  • No (e.g., DHA Lahore Phase 6+, ICCI Islamabad, some industrial zones): on-grid is enough. You won't notice missing backup. Save PKR 50,000 - 100,000.
  • Yes: continue to Q3. On-grid alone will frustrate you during outages.

Q3 — Are you applying for NEPRA net-metering (selling excess back to the grid)?

  • Yes: hybrid. On-grid can't keep your house running during outages; hybrid does both jobs.
  • No (small system, mostly self-consume): hybrid is still the safer pick for 2026 Pakistan unless you're truly remote.

Q4 — What's your battery budget?

  • PKR 0 (no battery): hybrid still works — runs grid-tied without battery, can add later. Don't waste money on off-grid; it requires battery to function.
  • PKR 80,000 - 250,000 (5-10 kWh lithium): hybrid with battery is the sweet spot for typical Pakistani 3-bed home with 1-2 ACs.

4. Off-grid: when it's actually the right answer

Off-grid only makes economic sense in three Pakistani scenarios:

  • No grid available: agricultural land, remote farmhouses, tubewell sites where bringing a meter costs PKR 200,000+.
  • Mobile installs: caravans, food trucks, fish-farms with no permanent grid connection.
  • Backup-critical sites: hospitals, server rooms where 100% solar isolation is preferred for security or noise reasons.

For typical urban Pakistan with K-Electric / LESCO / IESCO / GEPCO / FESCO grid available, hybrid beats off-grid on lifetime cost every time. Off-grid forces you to oversize battery (PKR 200,000+ extra) to handle multi-day low-irradiance stretches.

5. NEPRA 2026 net-metering — on-grid + hybrid only

NEPRA's 2026 net-metering regulations (prosumer rules effective FY2026) require:

  • Bi-directional smart meter (utility supplies; PKR 0 to PKR 35,000 depending on DISCO)
  • UL 1741 / IEEE 1547 certified inverter with anti-islanding protection
  • Single-phase systems ≤ 12 kW; 3-phase up to system contracted load
  • Net-meter sells back at category-specific buy-back rate (residential ~PKR 19/kWh, commercial ~PKR 27/kWh in 2026)

Off-grid inverters cannot be net-metered — they're physically isolated from the grid. Only on-grid and hybrid topologies qualify. See our NEPRA 2026 net-metering equipment checklist for complete approval requirements.

6. Required protection — same for all three architectures

Regardless of inverter type, NEPRA 2026 requires:

  • DC isolator between panels and inverter (4 amp rating per string)
  • AC MCB or MCCB between inverter and house DB (sized 1.25× inverter continuous output)
  • RCBO Type A or Type B (NEPRA 2026 requires Type B for systems with battery storage to detect DC residual currents) — see our RCBO Type AC vs A vs B guide
  • Type 2 SPD (surge protector) on both DC and AC sides per IEC 61643-31
  • Earthing — chassis and PV frame bonded to a dedicated rod with < 10 Ω resistance per IEC 62548

Skipping any one of these can fail your NEPRA inspection — budget PKR 18,000 - 32,000 for the complete protection set on a 5 kW system.

7. Charge controller note for off-grid + hybrid

Off-grid and hybrid inverters typically have a built-in MPPT charge controller. You don't need a separate one for normal residential systems. PWM-only controllers are obsolete for systems above 1 kW. See our PWM vs MPPT honest comparison for when the difference matters.

8. Pakistani installer notes (Hall Road / Liberty / Industrial Area pricing reality)

  • Branded premium hybrid inverters (German / Chinese tier-1, 5-year manufacturer warranty) hold their PKR resale value better than budget Chinese tier-3 brands — you can sell a 3-year-old premium hybrid for ~55% of new price, vs ~25% for budget
  • "Hybrid" labelling is ambiguous — some inverters call themselves hybrid but only offer battery-backup with no grid-tie capability. Verify UL 1741 / IEEE 1547 certification before paying
  • Pakistani 50 Hz grid + 220V nominal — check the inverter spec sheet for "AC output voltage range 200-250V, 47-53 Hz" (most modern units pass; older import lots may not)
  • Inverter heat de-rating — Pakistani summer 45°C+ shaded ambient means a 5 kW nameplate inverter delivers 4.2-4.5 kW in July afternoon. Oversize by 15-20% if you size to peak load

9. FAQ

Can I upgrade an on-grid inverter to hybrid later by adding a battery?

No. On-grid inverters are physically incapable of operating without the grid — UL 1741 anti-islanding shuts them off when grid drops. To add battery backup you replace the inverter entirely. If you might want battery in 2-3 years, buy hybrid today and run it grid-tied initially (cost difference PKR 30,000 - 80,000 vs full replacement PKR 150,000+ later).

Does a hybrid inverter run my whole house during load-shedding?

Only the loads on the EPS (Emergency Power Supply) output port, sized to your battery + inverter rating. Typical 5 kW hybrid with 5 kWh battery runs: 1 AC (1.5 ton), lights, fans, fridge, computer for 4-6 hours. Geysers, ovens, motor pumps usually excluded (drawn directly from grid input).

What inverter size matches a 6 kW solar panel array?

5 kW inverter is standard for 6 kW panels in Pakistan — DC over-sizing 1.15-1.25× is normal and clips only the peak 30 minutes of noon production. 6 kW inverter for 6 kW panels wastes inverter capacity (no PV ever delivers 100% nameplate due to losses).

How long does a hybrid inverter last in Pakistani conditions?

Premium German / Chinese tier-1 hybrid inverters: 10-12 years with proper ventilation. Budget tier-3 import units: 5-7 years before capacitor / MOSFET failure. Pakistani 45°C+ summers accelerate component aging — install in ventilated, shaded location ideally with chassis temperature < 35°C.

Can I run a 3-phase hybrid inverter on single-phase grid?

No. 3-phase units require 3-phase grid input. Pakistani single-phase residential connections (most homes) get single-phase inverters. 3-phase is for industrial / commercial connections with 3-phase WAPDA / KE meter. Check your bill — "PH-3" on the rate code means 3-phase eligible.
