2. How each works

PWM (Pulse Width Modulation)

The PWM controller pulses the connection between the panel and the battery rapidly, effectively "averaging" the panel voltage down to the battery voltage. The panel runs at battery voltage (not its peak power point), and the excess voltage is wasted as heat or as reduced current.

Result: if your battery is at 12V and your panel is putting out 18V at peak power, the PWM forces the panel down to 12V — you get 12V × panel current, not 18V × panel current. Roughly 70% efficiency overall.

MPPT (Maximum Power Point Tracking)

The MPPT controller continuously samples the panel voltage and current to find the maximum power point (typically Vmp × Imp). It then runs a DC-DC converter to take the panel's voltage (e.g. 18V) and convert it to the battery's required voltage (e.g. 14V at the float-charge stage) — at proportionally higher current.

Result: you extract the panel's full peak power. Roughly 95% efficiency.

3. When PWM is actually OK

  • Single 100W panel + 12V battery + small lighting load (caravan, boat, small RV)
  • System cost is the dominant constraint and the absolute energy difference doesn't matter
  • Panel Voc is very close to battery voltage (rare with modern panels)

4. When MPPT pays back

  • Any 200W+ system
  • Any panel where Voc > 25V (almost all modern panels)
  • Off-grid lighting + appliances + USB charging — where the 25% extra energy matters
  • Hybrid inverter systems (MPPT is usually integrated)

7. FAQ

PWM vs MPPT — which should I buy in Pakistan?

For anything beyond a single 100W panel: MPPT. For caravan/boat/single small panel: PWM is acceptable.

Cheapest PWM in Pakistan 2026?

Sky-King 30A at PKR 3,200. Works with 12V or 24V battery, single small panel.

Cheapest MPPT in Pakistan 2026?

EpEver Tracer 4210AN at PKR 18,500. 40A / 100V — handles up to 2 kW PV array.

Do hybrid inverters need a separate charge controller?

No — Inverex, Knox, Crown, Solis hybrid inverters all have MPPT built in (often dual MPPT inputs). Standalone controllers are only for off-grid battery-only systems without an inverter.

Will MPPT work with PWM-rated panels?

Yes — panel technology is the same. MPPT is just smarter about extracting the panel's available power. Any panel works with MPPT or PWM.
