1. Why tubewell motors fail in Pakistan

Three killers, in order of frequency:

  1. Voltage sag (45% of failures) — motor draws higher current at 195V; overload trips or windings overheat
  2. Single phasing (35% of failures) — one phase drops out; motor runs on 2 phases until burns
  3. Dry running (15%) — water table drops; pump sucks air; impellers melt
  4. Lightning + surge (5%) — nearby strike fries motor + inverter

4. Wiring sequence

  1. Power off at upstream isolator.
  2. Main MCB → voltage protector → phase failure relay (3-ph only).
  3. Then through magnetic contactor → thermal overload relay → to motor terminals.
  4. Control: start/stop push-buttons with overload NC and PFR NC in series with contactor coil.
  5. Optional: WiFi smart breaker between voltage protector and contactor for app control.
  6. SPD: at the panel input, with short earth wire to building earth.

5. FAQ

Do I need all 4-5 protection devices for a 1 HP residential pump?

Minimum: MCB + voltage protector + thermal overload. Add phase failure if 3-phase. Add WiFi smart breaker if you want app control + auto-cut after N hours runtime.

Will a CNC YC7VA voltage protector work with single-phase tubewell?

Yes — YC7VA series handles single-phase motors up to 80A.

What's the bare-minimum budget protection?

PKR 1,000-1,500: 1 MCB + 1 manual overload reset. Won't catch voltage sag or single phasing. Cheap insurance only.

Solar + tubewell combo — what changes?

If running off a solar VFD: the VFD provides built-in soft-start + speed control. You still need MCB + voltage protector (on supply input) + dry-run sensor. The contactor + thermal overload may be integrated in the VFD.
