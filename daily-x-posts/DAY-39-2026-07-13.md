## Tweet 2 (Pakistan thermal cycling problem)

Pakistan's biggest panel wiring hazard is invisible:

Lahore/Karachi panels heat to 55°C daily in summer → cool to 25°C at night.

That 30°C daily swing makes screw-type terminal block screws loosen over 2–4 years.

Loose screw → high resistance → heat → arc → fire.

Spring-clamp terminals maintain constant force at any temperature. 2/5

## Tweet 3 (WAPDA inspection failures)

5 reasons WAPDA rejects your commercial panel:

1. Bare strand ends in terminals (no ferrule)
2. Unlabelled terminal strips
3. No green/yellow PE ground terminals
4. Wiring duct fill >70%
5. Terminal strips without end clamps

All fixable for under Rs. 2,000 extra per panel.

Rs. 50,000 re-inspection delay is the alternative. 3/5

## Tweet 4 (EMC separation rule for Pakistan motor panels)

Pakistan motor drive panels (VFD + contactor + PLC):

Run signal wiring (4–20mA, RTD, I/O) in LEFT duct.
Run power wiring (230VAC, 415VAC, motor cables) in RIGHT duct.

Never mix in same duct.

EMC interference from 37kW VFD into a PT100 RTD loop creates unstable temperature readings → incorrect process control → product waste. 4/5
