## Post 2 — AC3 vs AC1 rating warning (high engagement: costly mistake alert)

🚨 The most expensive contactor mistake in Pakistan:

Buying a "25A contactor" and assuming it handles a 25A motor load.

Reality: the 25A label is the AC1 rating (resistive loads).
The AC3 rating (motor loads) is typically 9–12A on the same contactor.

AC1 on a motor circuit → contacts weld shut → motor runs unprotected → burnout.

Always check the AC3 column in the datasheet before you buy.

## Post 3 — Phase failure = silent motor killer (rural/agricultural audience)

Pakistan rural 3-phase supply kills more motors than anything else.

A blown fuse on one phase → motor keeps spinning on 2 phases → draws 180% current on remaining phases → trips thermal OLR in 20–60 minutes.

By then: winding insulation already degraded.

Fix: add a phase-failure relay alongside your contactor. Cost: PKR 800–2,500.
Motor rewind cost: PKR 15,000–80,000.

Do the math.

## Post 4 — DOL vs Star-Delta decision (practical + shareable)

DOL or Star-Delta? Quick Pakistan decision guide:

✅ DOL if:
— Motor ≤ 10 HP (7.5 kW)
— Strong urban WAPDA feeder
— Budget is tight (1 contactor + 1 OLR)

✅ Star-Delta if:
— Motor > 10 HP
— Rural agricultural connection (weak feeder)
— Starting current causing voltage dips on shared feeder

Star-Delta cost: ~3× DOL cost. Starting current: 33% of DOL.
