## Tweet 2 (Pakistan-specific — most important angle)

Pakistan solar owners: this is critical.

Your solar inverter generates DC leakage current during normal operation.

A Type AC RCBO CANNOT detect DC leakage.

If a fault develops on your inverter — the RCBO may never trip.
Live chassis. Fire risk. No warning.

Type A RCBO costs Rs. 1,200–Rs. 2,500 more.
It detects both AC AND DC leakage.

Install Type A on every solar circuit in Pakistan. Non-negotiable.

## Tweet 3 (EV charger angle — growing Pakistan market)

Setting up an EV charger in Pakistan?

Correct RCBO for 7kW AC charger:
✅ Type A (not Type AC)
✅ 40A rating
✅ C-curve (handles inrush)
✅ 30mA sensitivity
✅ Dedicated circuit only

Installing Type AC on an EV charger circuit is a fire and shock risk.
The charger's onboard electronics generate DC leakage — invisible to Type AC.

Rs. 3,000–Rs. 6,500 for the right RCBO.
Rs. 0 for the wrong one that fails silently.

## Tweet 4 (Pakistan DB space constraint — very practical)

Pakistan apartment DB board problem:

You have a 12-way consumer unit.
You want to add RCD protection to 6 circuits.

A separate RCCB + 6 MCBs = 6 (MCBs) + 2 (RCCB) = 8 modules. Fits.
BUT: 1 RCCB trip kills ALL 6 circuits.

6 individual RCBOs (1P+N, 2 modules each) = 12 modules.
No room for incomer MCB.

Solution: 16-way or 18-way consumer unit + 6 RCBOs.
Or: RCBO only on mandatory circuits (bathroom, kitchen, outdoor).

Plan your DB space BEFORE ordering. Guide link 👇

## Tweet 5 (Standalone — practical warning)

RCBO keeps tripping in Pakistan?

5 most common causes:

1️⃣ Old geyser with insulation breakdown (measure leakage current)
2️⃣ Type AC on solar/VFD/EV circuit → replace with Type A
3️⃣ B-curve on AC unit or motor → replace with C-curve
4️⃣ Monsoon moisture in outdoor conduit → seal junction boxes
5️⃣ Entry-tier RCBO from uncertified source → may trip randomly at wrong threshold

Don't reset and ignore a tripping RCBO.
It's telling you something.
