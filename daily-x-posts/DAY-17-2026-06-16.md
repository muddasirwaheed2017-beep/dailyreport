**Post 2 (CC vs CV):**
Constant Current (CC) vs Constant Voltage (CV) LED driver — simplified:

CC driver: holds mA fixed (e.g. 350 mA), voltage floats
→ Use for: downlights, panel lights, track lights, floodlights

CV driver: holds V fixed (12 V or 24 V), current floats
→ Use for: LED strips, cabinet lights, neon flex

Check the fixture label. If it says mA → CC driver. If it says VDC → CV driver.

**Post 3 (Pakistan-specific):**
Derating rule no one tells you in Pakistan:

A 50W driver at 45°C ambient (summer, false ceiling) runs at ~70% efficiency.

Load it above 80% → lifespan drops from 50,000 hrs to under 20,000.

Rule: 80% derating in ventilated space. 70% in sealed ceiling cavity.

For a 54W load in a false ceiling → use a 75W or 100W driver, not 60W.

---

**Post 4 (Dimmer warning):**
Dimmer switch + LED driver ≠ automatic dimming.

You need the right COMBINATION:

TRIAC dimmer → TRIAC dimmable driver ✅
0-10V panel dimmer → 0-10V driver ✅
DALI controller → DALI driver ✅

Mixing them = flicker, minimum brightness lock, or driver failure.

Most dimmer switches sold in Pakistan are TRIAC. Match accordingly.
