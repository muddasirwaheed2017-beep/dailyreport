### Post 2 (Thread — the 3 killers)

🧵 The 3 things that burn 3-phase motors in Pakistan — and what stops each one:

1/ Phase loss (single-phasing)
Motor keeps running on 2 phases = 173% rated current = burns in 3 minutes
→ Phase MONITORING relay trips in 0.1 seconds ✅

2/ Phase reversal
WAPDA reconnects after maintenance with wrong phase order
Motor runs backwards → pump delivers zero water, compressor delivers zero pressure
→ Phase SEQUENCE relay trips instantly ✅

3/ Low voltage
Rural Punjab/Sindh feeders drop to 320–340V during summer peak
Motor draws 17% excess current, runs 15°C hotter, insulation cracks after 2 seasons
→ Under-voltage relay trips at your set threshold ✅

A Rs 3,000 phase monitoring relay handles all three.

### Post 3 (Pakistan-specific angle — WAPDA tip)

WAPDA relay setting tip for Pakistan:

Don't set your under-voltage trip at 350V if you're on a rural feeder.

During load shedding restoration, WAPDA voltage often dips to 300–320V for 5–10 seconds before stabilising.

Set trip delay to 5–10 seconds (not 0.1 seconds) so the relay rides through the reconnection transient.

Your motor can survive 5 seconds of low voltage at no-load.
It cannot survive 3 minutes of single-phasing.

Prioritise: trip FAST on phase loss. Trip SLOW on voltage dip.
