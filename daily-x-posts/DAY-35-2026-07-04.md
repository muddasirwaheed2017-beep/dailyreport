**Tweet 2 — Pakistan-specific insight (high-engagement)**
🔥 The Pakistan busbar mistake that causes panel fires:

Busbar ampacity tables assume 35°C ambient.

Your switchroom in Lahore in June: 50°C.

De-rating factor at 50°C = 0.79×

50×5 mm copper busbar rated at 430A?
→ At 50°C it can only safely carry 340A

Spec your busbar for Pakistan summer ambient. Not IEC standard conditions.

---

**Tweet 3 — Installation tip (practical value)**
3 rules for busbar joints that DON'T cause fires 🔩

1. Torque: M8=20Nm, M10=40Nm, M12=70Nm (torque wrench — not "tight enough by hand")

2. Spring washers at every joint — load shedding thermal cycles loosen hand-tight joints in 3 months

3. Joint compound on aluminium — bare aluminium oxide = insulating layer = hot spot

Re-torque after 48 hours under load. Mark with witness paint.

### Standalone Tweet (best performer format)

Quick question for Pakistani panel builders:

What busbar do you use for a 200 kVA (415V 3-phase) transformer incomer?

Full-load current: 278A
+ 25% margin: 348A
De-rated at 45°C (×0.87): 348 ÷ 0.87 = 400A required

Correct answer: 50×5 mm copper (374A) — borderline → step up to 63×6 mm (505A de-rated)

Most panels in Pakistan are undersized for summer ambient.
