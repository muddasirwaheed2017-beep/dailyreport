## Post B — Technical (engagement)

Most Pakistani solar installers use 30° tilt year-round.

That's wrong.

Optimal tilt by city:
🌆 Karachi: 24° (summer) / 34° (winter)
🌆 Lahore: 28° / 38°
🌆 Islamabad: 30° / 42°
🌆 Multan: 26° / 36°

Correct seasonal tilt → 8–12% more generation. Don't leave free units on the roof.

## Post C — Pakistan-specific problem

Pakistani rooftop problem nobody talks about:

RCC slab: use chemical anchor bolts (not expansion bolts). Expansion bolts in concrete age 3–5 years — vibration from inverter cooling fans loosens them.

Tin/sheet roof: use 316 stainless steel hooks + EPDM washer. Galvanised iron rusts through in 2 monsoons.

IEC 62938 says so. Most local installers don't read IEC 62938.

## Post D — Engagement question

Quick poll for Pakistani solar owners:

Did your installer check the structural load of your roof before mounting panels?

Frame weight alone: 12–18 kg/m²
Wind uplift (Karachi coastal): +40 kg/m² additional

Most residential slabs are designed for 150 kg/m² live load. Panels are fine — IF anchor bolts are correct.
