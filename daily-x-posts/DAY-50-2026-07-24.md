### Tweet 2 (The problem)
Pakistan panel builders measure earth resistance in August (peak monsoon).

Pass at 4 Ω. Sign off the installation. Move on.

April arrives. Soil dries. Same rod: 35 Ω.

Earth leakage relay at 300 mA sensitivity: touch voltage = 300 mA × 35 Ω = 10.5 V.
That's fine.

But a bolted earth fault? 240 V / 35 Ω = 6.9 A.
Your 30 mA RCCB trips. Good.

The nuisance trips begin. Someone replaces the RCCB with a 300 mA unit "to stop the trips."

Now you have a problem.

### Tweet 5 (Bentonite treatment)
Bentonite is the single best upgrade you can make to a Pakistan earth rod installation.

It's a high-swelling clay. In sandy MEPCO soil, bentonite reduces earth resistance by 60–80%.

Application:
→ Bore a hole (not just drive the rod)
→ Pour bentonite slurry first
→ Drive rod through slurry
→ Backfill with dry bentonite powder

Cost: PKR 600–1,100 per 25 kg bag.

Worth it every time.

---

### Tweet 6 (Parallel rods — the spacing mistake)
Installing 2 earth rods at 1 m spacing doesn't halve your resistance.

It reduces it by maybe 10%.

The minimum spacing for parallel rods:
**2 × rod length**

3 m rods → 6 m minimum spacing.

At 6 m spacing, 2 rods reduce resistance by ~37%.
3 rods at 9 m spacing: ~55% reduction.

Rods too close share the same soil resistance zone. Space them properly.
