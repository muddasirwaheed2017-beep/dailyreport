**Tweet 2 (The Ics trap — most dangerous Pakistan MCCB mistake)**
The most dangerous MCCB mistake in Pakistan:

Buying by Icu instead of Ics.

"25 kA" MCCB on the box.
Ics = 6 kA in the datasheet.

If your network fault level is 15 kA — this breaker will CATASTROPHICALLY FAIL on the second fault.

The box won't save your panel. The Ics rating will.

Always demand the datasheet. Always check Ics.

**Tweet 3 (Temperature derating rule)**
Pakistan electrical rule nobody teaches in workshops:

Your 100A MCCB doesn't carry 100A in summer.

At 40°C (rated): 100A ✅
At 45°C: ~95A
At 50°C (typical enclosed panel, July–August): ~88A
At 55°C (no panel ventilation): ~80A

Standard MCCBs are rated at 40°C.
Your Karachi panel hits 50°C in July.

Pakistan rule: Always select one rating step UP.
100A load → install 125A or 160A MCCB.

One step. No calculations. Full headroom.

**Tweet 4 (Generator changeover neutral mistake)**
Pakistan generator panels: the 4-pole MCCB mistake

If your generator has its own earthing electrode, its neutral is ISOLATED from WAPDA neutral.

Using a 3-pole MCCB on generator changeover creates a parallel neutral path — eliminates isolation protection.

Fix: 4-pole MCCB for generator incomers.
Switch ALL FOUR conductors (R, S, T + N).

The extra pole costs Rs. 2,000–5,000 more.
The fault it prevents costs your entire panel.
