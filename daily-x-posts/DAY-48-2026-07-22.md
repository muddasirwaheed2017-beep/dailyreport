**Tweet 2 (The Problem):**
NEMA's rule of thumb:
1% voltage unbalance → 6–10% CURRENT unbalance.

At 3% voltage asymmetry (common on WAPDA summer feeders):
→ 15–25% current unbalance
→ One winding takes 18°C of extra heat
→ Thermal OL averages all 3 phases — sees nothing

The motor "runs fine" until it doesn't. (2/8)

**Tweet 4 (The Relay):**

**Tweet 5 (Trip Settings):**
Pakistan DOL motor trip settings:

Under-voltage trip: 330V (87% of 380V)
Over-voltage trip: 430V (113%)
Asymmetry trip: 3% for motors ≥11kW
Time delay: 3–5 seconds (filters WAPDA switching transients)
Return delay: 5–10 seconds (waits for supply to stabilise)

⚠️ Don't use 0s delay — every load-shedding return will nuisance-trip. (5/8)

**Tweet 6 (Auto-restart Warning):**
Auto-restart vs Manual-reset — get this wrong and someone gets hurt.

✅ Auto-restart: pumps, fans, HVAC — fine to auto-restart after load-shedding
❌ Manual reset: lathes, grinders, mills — unexpected restart = injury

PSQCA and ILO both require manual reset for rotating tooling.

Most Pakistan panels are wired wrong on this. (6/8)

---

**Tweet 7 (Wire It Right):**
One wiring mistake kills your protection:

❌ WRONG: Sensing terminals on motor side of contactor
✅ RIGHT: Sensing terminals on supply side of contactor

Motor side shows back-EMF during coast-down — gives false readings, misses supply faults.

Supply side only. Always. (7/8)
