**Tweet 2 — Pakistan's AC-23A trap**

The Rs 200 cam switch vs the Rs 700 cam switch — both say "25A."

The cheap one is AC-21A (resistive loads only).
The correct one is AC-23A (motor switching duty).

AC-23A can break 6–8× FLC (locked-rotor current).
AC-21A cannot.

Result of wrong selection: welded contacts, replacement within 3 months.

Look for AC-23A stamped on the body. If it's not there — don't use it for a motor panel.

**Tweet 3 — Pakistan voltage derating rule**

Why you should ALWAYS go one size up on cam switch ratings in Pakistan:

WAPDA feeder voltage drops to 340V during peak demand.
At 340V, a motor rated for 15A at 400V draws 18–19A.

Apply 1.25× derating → need 23–24A → 40A standard rating for a 10 HP motor.

Using a 25A cam switch on a 10 HP motor in Pakistan = contact failure within 12–18 months.

**Tweet 4 — The overload relay wiring error that bypasses protection**

Common wiring mistake in Pakistani pump panels:

Overload relay NC contact wired AFTER the cam switch LOCAL output.
Result: in REMOTE mode, a motor overload does NOT stop the motor.
The pump burns. Nobody knows until they check.

Fix: wire overload NC in the COMMON supply line to the cam switch — before the positions.
This way, overload trips cut power regardless of which position the handle is in.

## Standalone Tweet

WAPDA tubewell panel standard (Punjab + Sindh) requires a 3-position LOCAL/OFF/REMOTE selector switch rated 25A minimum for DOL panels.

The REMOTE position connects to the float switch. LOCAL overrides it for manual test.

Without the cam switch, operators wire/unwire the float switch circuit live.
That's why WAPDA draws specify it.

Don't substitute with relays — fails WAPDA inspection.
