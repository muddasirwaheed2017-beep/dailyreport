### Post 2 — Technical hook (engineer/installer audience)

Most Pakistani farmers buying a solar pump controller make the same mistake:

They size the PV array at 1:1 with motor kW.

The controller then only delivers full torque at solar noon — maybe 2 hours.

Correct rule: **PV kWp = 1.3–1.5 × motor kW**

A 5 HP (3.7 kW) motor → 5–5.5 kWp panels.
A 10 HP (7.5 kW) motor → 10–11 kWp panels.

The extra panels pay back in water pumped per day for the lifetime of the system.

### Post 3 — Question-format (organic reach)

Q: AC solar pump VFD or DC solar pump controller — which is right for a Pakistani tubewell?

If you already have an AC motor in the borehole:
→ MPPT solar pump VFD (reuse motor, add changeover for WAPDA backup)

If it's a brand-new small off-grid installation under 3 HP:
→ DC solar pump controller (slightly higher efficiency, simpler circuit)

The DC controller is more efficient but the DC motor is harder to source and repair locally.

90% of Pakistan tubewell conversions = AC VFD route.
