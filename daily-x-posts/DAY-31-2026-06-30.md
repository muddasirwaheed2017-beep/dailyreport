### Post 2 — LDR vs Photocell — The Confusion Pakistani Buyers Get Wrong

LDR switch ≠ photocell switch.

LDR = bare component. Cannot switch a lamp directly.

Photocell switch = LDR + relay circuit + housing = plug and play.

Asking for an "LDR switch" at a hardware shop will often get you just the resistor. Always say: photocell switch, dawn-to-dusk switch, or automatic light controller.

### Post 3 — Lux Settings for Pakistan

Photocell threshold guide for Pakistan 2026:

Clear sky (Islamabad, KPK): Turn ON at 10–15 lux | Turn OFF at 50–60 lux
Lahore / Karachi (urban): Turn ON at 15–25 lux | Turn OFF at 60–80 lux
Dusty areas (Faisalabad, Multan): Turn ON at 25 lux | Turn OFF at 80–100 lux
Monsoon afternoons: Add 30–60 sec time delay to prevent cloud-cover false triggering

Calibrate once. Label the pot setting. Save it in photos.

### Post 4 — Fatal Wiring Mistake Pakistani Electricians Make

Do NOT wire the photocell in the neutral conductor.

Some electricians do it to avoid touching the live wire. Result: the lamp fitting stays permanently live even when switched OFF by the photocell.

Danger: Electrocution when servicing a lamp that appears to be off.

Always switch the live (black/brown) conductor. Never switch neutral.
