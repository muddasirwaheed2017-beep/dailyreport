**Tweet 2 (the coordination problem):**
Type 1 and Type 2 SPDs in the same DB board = no coordination.

The surge wave reaches BOTH simultaneously. The Type 2 absorbs energy it was never rated for.

It dies. The Type 1 survived. You have zero downstream protection. And you don't know it.

---

**Tweet 3 (the 10-metre rule):**
IEC 60364-5-534 fix: put 10 metres of cable between Type 1 and Type 2.

In most Pakistan buildings this is already done naturally:
• Type 1 at WAPDA meter (boundary wall)
• Type 2 at MDB (inside building)
= 15–30m cable run ✅

But if they're in the same room — you have a problem.

---

**Tweet 4 (line inductor):**
Solution for compact panels where both SPDs are close together:

Line inductor — 1.5–2 µH per pole — installed between Type 1 output and Type 2 input.

Cost in Pakistan 2026:
• Single-phase: PKR 1,500–2,800
• Three-phase: PKR 4,200–7,500

A PKR 7,500 inductor protects a PKR 500,000 VFD. The maths work.

---

**Tweet 5 (backup MCB — the forgotten part):**
Every SPD also needs a backup MCB in series on the live conductor.

Not for normal operation — for when the SPD fails short-circuit after exhausting its energy capacity.

Without backup protection: the failed SPD becomes a permanent phase-to-earth fault. Wiring overheats.

Size: 63A Type C for Type 2. 125A Type C for Type 1+2 combined.

**Tweet 7 (monitoring — the most ignored part):**
SPD monitoring schedule for Pakistan monsoon:

• May: pre-monsoon inspection — replace red-status units before June
• Monthly June–September: check after every major storm
• October: post-monsoon closeout

A green indicator = thermal disconnect NOT triggered. Not = full capacity remaining.

After 5 monsoon seasons in Zone 1: replace anyway.
