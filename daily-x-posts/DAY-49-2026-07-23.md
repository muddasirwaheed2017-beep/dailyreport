**Tweet 2 — Pakistan Earthing System Types**
Pakistan has 3 earthing systems — and getting it wrong means no earth fault protection at all:

🟢 TN-C-S (urban LESCO/FESCO commercial): MCBs can clear earth faults. RCCB = backup + personal protection.

🔴 TT (MEPCO/PESCO/HESCO rural): Earth fault current is 7–24 A. MCB WILL NOT trip. RCCB is your ONLY protection.

Know which system feeds your site before specifying any protective device.

---

**Tweet 3 — Ground Fault Current Calculation**
In a WAPDA TT rural site with R_E = 10 Ω:

Earth fault current = 240 / (10 + 20) = 8 A

An MCB trips at 5–10× rated current. An 80 A MCB needs 400–800 A to trip in 0.1 seconds.

8 A fault runs for hours undetected. Cable burns. Motor fails. Insulation carbonises.

Calculate the fault current before assuming MCBs protect your site.

---

**Tweet 4 — Sensitivity Selection**
30 mA = Personal protection (shock) ← REQUIRED at all Pakistan socket circuits
100 mA = Fire protection in cable runs
300 mA = Equipment protection ONLY — does NOT protect persons

The fatal mistake: installing 300 mA at a socket outlet circuit.

IEC 60364 + PESC requirement: ≤30 mA at all Pakistan socket outlets. No exceptions.

---

**Tweet 5 — RCCB vs ELR**
RCCB: self-contained up to 125 A. Built-in breaking. Final circuits.

ELR: relay only. Requires external contactor/MCCB + toroid CT. Unlimited current rating.

For WAPDA load-shedding nuisance trips:
→ RCCB: can't add time delay (protection function)
→ ELR: adjustable 100–1,000 ms time delay to filter WAPDA restoration inrush

Industrial Pakistan feeders >125 A: always ELR + toroid CT.

---

**Tweet 6 — Toroid CT Wiring Rule**
Pass L1 + L2 + L3 + Neutral through the toroid CT.
Do NOT pass the Earth conductor through the toroid.

If you pass the earth conductor through the toroid, the fault current cancels the live-conductor imbalance — the ELR never trips even on a solid earth fault.

This wiring mistake is common in Pakistan panels and is fatal.
