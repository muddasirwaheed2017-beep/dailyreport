**Thread 2/4**

WAPDA metering rule Pakistani engineers get wrong every time 👇

❌ You CANNOT use a protection CT (5P10/5P20) for revenue metering.
❌ You CANNOT use a 1.0 class CT for WAPDA billing.

✅ WAPDA requires class 0.5S minimum for all LT industrial revenue metering CTs.

The "S" means accuracy is maintained down to 1% of rated current — critical for low-load billing accuracy.

**Thread 3/4**

The most expensive CT mistake in Pakistan panel building:

Using 1.5mm² cable for CT secondary wiring on a 5VA-rated CT.

At 10m run = 12VA burden on a 5VA CT → permanent saturation → all meter readings are systematically LOW.

Use 2.5mm² copper for all CT secondary wiring. Always.

The PKR 200 you save on cable costs you thousands in WAPDA over-billing per year.

**Thread 4/4**

CT ratio sizing rule:

Pick a ratio where your maximum load = 60–100% of CT primary rating.

❌ 160A load on a 100A/5A CT → CT saturates at 100A → wrong readings above that
✅ 160A load → use 200A/5A CT ✓

And ALWAYS match the CT ratio to what's programmed in your energy meter.
Mismatched ratio = readings 4× too high or 0.25× too low.
