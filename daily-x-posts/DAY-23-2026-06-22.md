## Post 2 — Curve Mistake Hook

The most common MCB mistake in Pakistan 🧵

Someone installs a 16A B-curve on their 1-ton AC.
AC keeps tripping.
They install a 32A B-curve "to stop the tripping."

Now the MCB won't trip — because 32A > the wire's safe current rating.
Next step: wiring fire.

The fix isn't a bigger MCB. It's the right CURVE.

B-curve = lighting (3–5× In trip)
C-curve = AC, fridges, motors (5–10× In)
D-curve = heavy industrial motors (10–20× In)

## Post 3 — Breaking Capacity Warning

Pakistani bazaar MCBs with no Icn marking ⚠️

That number in a rectangle on the MCB face (6000, 10000)?
That's the breaking capacity — the fault current it can safely interrupt.

No number = no guarantee.

Under a high fault current, an unrated MCB doesn't trip.
It explodes. Arc flash. Fire.

The difference between unrated and 6kA rated?
PKR 100–200 per MCB.

Never buy unrated MCBs for fixed wiring.

## Post 4 — 4P MCB Clarification

When do you NEED a 4-pole MCB in Pakistan?

✅ Main incoming breaker on a 3-phase supply
✅ Before any ATS / generator changeover switch
✅ TN-S earthing systems (neutral must be switched)
✅ Any point where you need to isolate all 4 conductors (3 phase + neutral)

❌ 3-phase motor sub-circuits → 3P is fine
❌ Single-phase circuits → 1P or 2P only

Using a 3P as main incoming on 3-phase = neutral stays live even with breaker off. Dangerous.
